# Equipment

## The Paddle

Pickleball paddles are solid (no strings) and must meet USAP specifications for tournament play.

### Materials

| Material | Feel | Best For |
|----------|------|----------|
| Graphite / Carbon fiber | Light, stiff, responsive | Control-oriented players |
| Fiberglass (composite) | Softer, more power | Power players, beginners |
| Wood | Heavy, durable | Casual/recreational play |

### Key Specs

- **Length:** max 17 in
- **Width:** max 7½ in
- **Total length + width:** max 24 in
- **Thickness:** typically 13 mm or 16 mm (thicker = more control)
- **Weight:** typically 7–8.5 oz (lighter = faster hands, heavier = more power)
- **Grip circumference:** 4–4½ in

### Choosing a Paddle

- **Beginners:** mid-weight composite, 16 mm core
- **Control players:** heavier, thicker, smaller sweet spot
- **Power players:** lighter, thinner, larger sweet spot

## The Ball

Pickleballs are perforated plastic balls, similar to a Wiffle ball.

- **Outdoor balls:** smaller, rounder holes (typically 40 holes); harder and faster
- **Indoor balls:** larger holes (typically 26 holes); softer, slower, more control

Approved balls are listed on the USAP website. Common brands: Dura, Franklin, Onix.

## The Court

- **Dimensions:** 20 ft wide × 44 ft long (same as a doubles badminton court)
- **Net height:** 36 in at sidelines, 34 in at center
- **Non-volley zone (kitchen):** 7 ft on each side of the net
- **Service boxes:** each side is divided into left and right service areas

A standard tennis court can fit **two** pickleball courts side by side.

## Footwear

- Use **court shoes** (tennis or pickleball-specific) — running shoes lack lateral support
- Look for durability on the toe box (dragging during the kitchen game wears them fast)

## Optional Gear

- Overgrip tape — extends grip life and customizes feel
- Paddle bag or backpack
- Sweatbands, sun visor, sunglasses for outdoor play
- Knee/elbow braces as needed
