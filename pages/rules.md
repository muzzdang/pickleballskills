# Rules

## The Basics

Pickleball is played to **11 points**, win by **2**. Tournament games may go to 15 or 21. Only the **serving side** can score a point.

## Serving

- Serve must be hit **underhand** with paddle contact below the wrist
- Serve diagonally cross-court into the opponent's service box
- Both feet must be behind the baseline at the moment of contact
- One serve attempt per rally (no second serve, except on a let)
- Server calls the score before each serve: **server score – receiver score – server number** (doubles)

## The Double-Bounce Rule

After the serve:
1. The receiving team must let the ball **bounce once** before returning
2. The serving team must then let the return **bounce once** before playing it
3. After these two bounces, either team may volley or play off the bounce

This rule prevents serve-and-volley dominance and extends rallies.

## The Non-Volley Zone (Kitchen)

- The 7-foot zone on each side of the net is called the **kitchen**
- You may **not** volley (hit the ball out of the air) while standing in the kitchen or on its lines
- You may enter the kitchen to play a ball that has bounced
- If your momentum carries you into the kitchen after a volley, the fault stands even if you already hit the ball

## Faults

A fault ends the rally. Common faults:

- Ball lands out of bounds
- Ball hits the net
- Volleying from the kitchen
- Volleying before the double-bounce rule is satisfied
- Ball bounces twice before being hit
- Player or clothing touches the net

## Scoring in Doubles

Score is called as three numbers: **serving team score – receiving team score – server number (1 or 2)**.

At the start of a game, the first serving team only gets **one server** (to reduce the advantage of serving first). After that, both players on each team serve before the serve passes to the other team.

## Line Calls

- A ball touching any part of a boundary line is **in**
- A serve that lands on the **kitchen line** is a **fault**
- Players are responsible for calling their own side of the court
