# Scoring

## Core Scoring Rules

- Games are typically played to **11 points**, win by **2**
- Tournament games may be played to **15 or 21**, win by **2**
- Only the **serving team** can score a point (rally scoring is sometimes used in recreational play but is not official USAP rules)

## Doubles Scoring (Official)

Score is announced as three numbers before every serve:

> **Serving team score – Receiving team score – Server number**

**Example:** `6 – 4 – 2` means the serving team has 6, receiving team has 4, and it's the second server's turn.

### Server Rotation

1. When the serving team wins a rally, the **same server** continues (switching sides)
2. When the serving team faults, the **second server** takes over (no side switch)
3. When the second server faults, the serve passes to the opposing team (**side out**)
4. Both players on the new serving team get a turn before another side out

### First Serve Exception

At the very start of a game, the first serving team begins with **only one server** (called "server 2" or "0-0-2") to reduce the statistical advantage of serving first.

## Singles Scoring

Score is two numbers: **server score – receiver score**.

- Server serves from the **right side** when their score is even
- Server serves from the **left side** when their score is odd

## Rally Scoring (Recreational Variant)

Some recreational players use rally scoring where **every rally** scores a point regardless of who served. This speeds up games significantly but is not sanctioned for official play.

## Keeping Score — Tips

- **Always announce score before serving** — it's the server's responsibility
- If there is a dispute, replay the point
- Use a visible scoreboard when available (tournaments always use one)

## Match Formats

| Format | Games | Points |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Recreational | Best of 1 | 11, win by 2 |
| Tournament standard | Best of 3 | 11, win by 2 |
| Gold medal / final | Best of 3 | 15, win by 2 |
| Some tournaments | Best of 3 | 21, win by 2 |
