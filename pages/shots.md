# Shots

## The Dink

The dink is the defining shot of pickleball — a soft, arcing shot that lands in the opponent's kitchen.

- **Purpose:** force opponents to hit up, preventing them from attacking
- **Technique:** short compact swing, paddle face open, contact out in front
- **Key:** keep it low over the net and close to the kitchen line
- **When to use:** during kitchen battles (the "dinking game") to wait for an attackable ball

## The Drop Shot (Third-Shot Drop)

The third-shot drop is arguably the most important shot to master.

- **Purpose:** neutralize the net advantage after the serve — allows the serving team to advance to the kitchen
- **Technique:** soft, controlled shot hit from the baseline or mid-court that arcs into the opponent's kitchen
- **Key:** must land in the kitchen; too high and opponents will attack it
- **When to use:** as the serving team's third shot (serve → return → **drop**)

## The Drive

A hard, flat, low shot hit with pace.

- **Purpose:** apply pressure, force errors, or create an attackable pop-up
- **When to use:** when opponent's shot sits up, or as an alternative to the third-shot drop
- **Risk:** a drive that is too high will be put away by net players

## The Lob

A high, deep shot designed to send opponents scrambling back.

- **Purpose:** move opponents off the kitchen line, create space, reset pressure
- **Technique:** open paddle face, upward swing, aim for deep baseline
- **Risk:** if too short, opponents will smash it; wind can affect trajectory outdoors
- **When to use:** when opponents are camped at the kitchen and leaning forward

## The Overhead / Smash

An aggressive downward strike on a high ball.

- **Purpose:** put away a lob or any high floating ball
- **Technique:** continental grip, get behind the ball, hit flat and down
- **Key:** move your feet to position before swinging

## The Volley

Hitting the ball out of the air before it bounces (not allowed in the kitchen).

- **Punch volley:** short, firm punch — used at the kitchen line
- **Roll volley:** topspin volley used to attack a mid-level ball
- **Reset volley:** soft absorbing volley to slow down a hard-driven ball

## The Erne

An advanced shot where a player leaps around or over the kitchen corner to volley from outside the NVZ.

- **Purpose:** surprise opponents with an unexpected angle
- **Legal requirement:** feet must not touch the kitchen; player typically jumps from outside the NVZ sideline

## The ATP (Around-the-Post)

Hitting the ball around the outside of the net post rather than over the net.

- **When possible:** when the ball is pulled wide beyond the sideline
- **Legal:** the ball does not need to travel over the net — it can go around the post at any height
- **Spectacular and rare** — one of the highlight plays in pickleball

## Spin Types

| Spin | How to Apply | Effect |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Topspin | Brush upward on contact | Ball dips down, bounces high |
| Backspin (slice) | Brush downward on contact | Ball stays low, skids |
| Sidespin | Brush across | Ball curves, bounces sideways |
