# Strategy

## The Golden Rule: Get to the Kitchen

The team controlling the kitchen (non-volley zone line) wins most rallies. Your primary goal on every point is to reach the kitchen line as quickly and safely as possible.

## The 3-Shot Pattern (Serving Team)

1. **Serve** — deep, controlled, toward the backhand corner
2. **Wait** — let the return bounce (double-bounce rule)
3. **Third-shot drop** — arc it softly into the opponent's kitchen, then advance

The third-shot drop buys time to move from the baseline to the kitchen line.

## The 3-Shot Pattern (Receiving Team)

1. **Return deep** — hit the return deep to push the server back, then advance to the kitchen
2. **Get to the kitchen** — you have the advantage; reach the line immediately after returning
3. **Dink or attack** — wait patiently for an attackable ball

## Kitchen Line Positioning

- Stand close to but not on the kitchen line
- Keep paddle up, in front of your body ("up and ready")
- Move laterally together with your partner (doubles) — stay in sync

## Stacking (Doubles)

Stacking is a positioning strategy where both players line up on the same side before the serve or return, then shift after contact. Used to keep a specific player on their preferred side regardless of score-based positioning.

- **Why:** keep the stronger player (or preferred forehand side) in the middle
- **How:** one player serves or returns from the "wrong" box, the other covers the gap

## Targeting in Doubles

- **Hit to the middle:** creates confusion over who takes the ball, reduces angles
- **Attack feet:** balls at the opponent's feet are very hard to return cleanly
- **Target the weaker player:** identify and exploit the weaker side
- **Change the pace:** mixing drives with drops and dinks prevents opponents from settling

## Patience vs. Aggression

- **Be patient** in dink rallies — wait for a ball above net height before attacking
- **Be aggressive** when given a high, slow ball
- **Reset** when in trouble — a soft, low return buys time to regroup

## Handling Bangers (Power Players)

Some opponents try to overpower with hard drives rather than dinking.

- **Block and reset:** use a soft, absorbing grip to redirect pace into the kitchen
- **Stay low:** bend your knees so you don't pop the ball up
- **Take pace off:** let the ball do the work — don't swing back at a banger

## Wind and Outdoor Adjustments

- Hit harder into the wind; ease up when wind is at your back
- Keep lobs to a minimum on windy days
- Aim lower over the net — wind will carry the ball more than expected

## Communication in Doubles

- Call "mine," "yours," or "bounce it" on every ball you're unsure about
- After each rally, briefly discuss what happened
- Decide before the match who takes the middle — usually the forehand player
