# Attack Trigger

Tyler's single most repeated principle across all 49 videos: **only attack when contact is at or above the net**. Earn the attack — do not force it.

## The Red-Light / Green-Light System

**Green light** (attack): Ball is at or above net height. You are in a dominant position. Contact is out in front of and away from your body.

**Red light** (block or reset): Ball is below the net. You do not have a dominant position. Ball is close to or behind your body.

**No yellow light.** This is the most important refinement from the May 2026 kitchen domination video: *"The moment you think yellow light is the moment you hesitate. In pickleball, time is a currency. If you hesitate, you're automatically wrong."* Black or white. No gray area.

## The Two Attack Triggers

Tyler teaches two separate trigger conditions (from the Offensive Masterclass, Feb 2026):

1. **High ball above net height.** The obvious trigger most players know.
2. **Ball away from your body — space available.** Even if the ball is below net height, if there is space between the ball and your body, you *may* be able to be aggressive. If the ball is jammed close to your body, all you can do is block. This is why players step *back* to create space before a speed-up.

## The Setup Prerequisite

Attacks don't come from nowhere — they are earned through rally construction:

- **At the kitchen:** Dink to move the opponent side-to-side until they pop a ball up. That pop-up is your trigger. Don't speed up on everything — wait for the right ball.
- **On drives:** The 3rd shot drive at 60–70% forces a weak block. You advance to the kitchen. The attack opportunity comes on the 5th or 7th shot, not the 3rd.
- **On returns:** A deep, aggressive return often creates a weak 3rd shot. Moving up after the return positions you to attack that weak response.

## Commitment is Strategic Information

Tyler is emphatic: **tap-backs train opponents not to fear popping up.** "If you get a high drop and just tap it back, you're telling me I can pop up forever." A half-hearted response signals the opponent they have nothing to fear. When the trigger fires, commit.

Conversely: commitment to aggression is not recklessness. A missed attack is always 100% your opponent's point. Aim inside target zones, not at lines. Be willing to hit two or three aggressive shots rather than trying to end it on the first.

## Application by Situation

**Dinking rally:** Wait for ball at or above net. Side-to-side dinking creates this opportunity. See [Kitchen Dominance](../concepts/kitchen-dominance.md).

**Speed-up:** Step back to create space, contact out in front and away. Flat swing. Aim at opponent's chest or feet. See [Offensive Roll Volley](../concepts/offensive-roll-volley.md).

**Put-away volley:** Elbow tucked, wrist snap down. Finish center-front of body. See [Kitchen Dominance](../concepts/kitchen-dominance.md).

**After good serve/return/drop:** Recognize when the setup is working and move forward to capitalize. See [Drive-Drop Sequence](../concepts/drive-drop-sequence.md).
