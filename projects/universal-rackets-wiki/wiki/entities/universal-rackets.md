Provides the student/learner perspective, demonstrates drills alongside Tyler, and offers her own coaching cues (e.g., "You'll never be a 4.0 if you can't hit the third shot drop"). Her presence creates a more conversational, clinic-style dynamic in videos.

## Teaching Philosophy

Universal Rackets is built around a coherent system — not isolated tips. Tyler's core principles:

- **Fundamentals over power.** Master body position, contact point, and swing path before adding pace. Most problems at club level are positioning problems, not technique problems.
- **Spin with purpose.** Three spins, three jobs: topspin for drives/serves (insurance policy — same swing, ball dips into court); slice for drops/returns (keep ball low, change pace); flat only for speedups and put-aways where you are fully dominant. See [Topspin-Flat-Slice Framework](../concepts/topspin-flat-slice-framework.md).
- **Offense on your terms.** The red-light/green-light rule: only attack when contact is at or above the net. Below the net = block. Earn it, don't force it. See [Attack Trigger](../concepts/attack-trigger.md).
- **Footwork fixes everything.** "Every problem in pickleball is a footwork problem." Popping up? You're reaching. Can't get to the kitchen? Flat-footed. Getting jammed? Not setting up first. See [Footwork System](../concepts/footwork-system.md).
- **Placement beats power.** Drive at 60–70%, not 100%. A ball that's in always gives the opponent a chance to miss. "Put the ball where your opponent isn't." See [Error Reduction](../concepts/error-reduction.md).
- **Catchphrase: "Buckle your seat belts."** Tyler's signal that a drill or concept is about to demand full commitment.

## Recurring Cues

- "Put the car in drive before hitting the gas" — take the paddle back BEFORE moving to the ball
- "Hit through three balls, not one" — maintain extension through contact
- "Leave room for the Holy Ghost" — keep space between paddle and body at contact
- "Smell your armpits" — finish the backhand follow-through with elbows pointing up
- "Hug a tree / air out your armpits" — paddle extended out front in ready position
- "Cornhole toss / bowling / beanbag throw" — the third shot drop motion
- "Paddle as measuring stick" — extend paddle to decide volley vs. bounce
- "Sit on the toilet, then stand up" — the drip/topspin swing path
- "Visualize the ball skimming the net" — dink cue to avoid popping up
- "Red light, green light" — at/above net = attack; below net = block
