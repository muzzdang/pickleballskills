## Key Takeaways

Tyler's three-level framework — crawl, walk, run, sprint — applied to the serve regardless of stance or motion:

- **Level 1 — Synchronize toss and swing.** Drop-and-catch drill: put the paddle down, hold ball at 1:00 (righty) or 11:00 (lefty), release it and catch. Repeat until automatic. Then add the paddle: start with paddle back, ball out front, drop, hit, follow through. Say "drop, hit" aloud to build muscle memory. No bounce serves — these create a skill ceiling.
- **Level 2 — Add rotation and acceleration.** Face the right net post (right-handed), not the target. This creates rotation. "Rotation plus acceleration equals power." Loose grip (scale of 1-10, aim for 2-3): squeeze with index finger and thumb only to learn the loose feeling. "To gain control I have to give up control — let the paddle work for you."
- **Level 3 — Legs and momentum.** Load into the back leg, then explode forward. Two options: (a) start forward, rock back, go forward; (b) start back, go forward. The moment you push off the back leg is the moment you fire the paddle. Let momentum carry the body forward after contact. Wherever you point your body, the ball goes.
- **The mantra:** "Elbow points to the target" — wherever the elbow finishes after the follow-through indicates direction.
- **Serves enabled by this foundation:** deep, short-angle, slice, lob — all accessible once L1–L3 are solid.

## Visual Notes

Tyler demonstrates from both open and closed stance, showing the back-foot pivot (squashing the bug) that releases the hips. He demonstrates the C-swing path: back = top of C, drop = curve of C, forward finish = check mark. Body forward/head down cue shown as essential to accuracy.
