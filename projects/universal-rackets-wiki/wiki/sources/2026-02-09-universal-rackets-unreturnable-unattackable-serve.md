# How to Hit an Unreturnable and Unattackable Serve (Universal Rackets)

**Source:** YouTube
**Channel:** [Universal Rackets](../entities/universal-rackets.md)

## Key Takeaways

Tyler and Michele co-host this comprehensive serve strategy masterclass:

- **Read your opponent before serving.** What's their weakness? Are they mobile or static? Do they run around their backhand? Do they leave their forehand side open? Are they an early mover or late reactor? Your serve answer is different for every opponent.
- **Target the backhand.** 80% of players prefer their forehand. Right-handed players: serve to the right side of the box from both service positions.
- **Serve directly at the body.** Often overlooked — aiming at the opponent's hip/chest jams them. They can't step around it and they can't get swing space. Creates a short return almost every time at club level.
- **Depth is greater than power.** Swing from 6 to 12 o'clock (upward). "Get into your legs, lift the ball, follow through with your elbow pointing directly to where you want it to land." Play with height and topspin — the ball will still dive in.
- **The "unattackable" formula:** deep + topspin + to backhand = ball lands deep, kicks up off the bounce, goes to the weaker side. Forces a defensive return.
- **Variety is key — serve like a pitcher.** Have: deep topspin, flat power serve, short-angle, lob serve. Change up throughout a match.
- **Slice serve cue:** open paddle face (like hitting a low dink), swing to the LEFT (for a righty). The ball curves opposite the swing direction.
- **Topspin serve cue:** "sideways wave" or "petting the dog" — never turn the wrist/unlock-the-door, just wave sideways-diagonally while swinging low to high.
- **Lob serve:** especially effective with sun in opponents' eyes. "If you miss a lob serve you should just go home."
- **Drill for depth:** place cones in a line 4 feet from the kitchen. Must serve beyond the line. Progress until serving to within 2 feet of the baseline.
