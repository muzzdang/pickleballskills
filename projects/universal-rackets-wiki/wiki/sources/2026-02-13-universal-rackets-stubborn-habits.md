# Stubborn Pickleball Habits Losing You Matches (Universal Rackets)

**Source:** YouTube
**Channel:** [Universal Rackets](../entities/universal-rackets.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fPSnEeSxtfA
**Duration:** 35:06
**Uploaded:** 2026-02-13

## Key Takeaways

Tyler and Michele co-host. Specific bad habits called out with corrections:

1. **No ready position.** After hitting, paddle drops down. Fix: after every shot, touch the paddle with the non-dominant hand to force the return to ready position.
2. **Not knowing how to transition forehand ↔ backhand.** Players get stuck in forehand grip and reach around for wide balls. Fix: "the paddle center of body is a magnet" — always return to neutral ready position after each shot.
3. **Watching the shot instead of following the ball.** After hitting, players freeze and admire their shot. Fix: follow the ball with your body. If you hit crosscourt, move crosscourt.
4. **Wrong paddle tilt.** (a) Incorrect grip entirely. (b) Same paddle face angle for all contact heights — open face for high balls sends them out; closed face for low balls sends them into the net. Fix: low contact = open face; high contact = closed face.
5. **Running with paddle down.** When transitioning from baseline to kitchen, paddle should rise as you approach — not stay down. The closer to the kitchen, the higher the paddle.
6. **Not communicating with partner.** Silence causes middle-ball miscommunication. Fix: say "mine" or "yours" even for obvious balls.
7. **Panic after popping the ball up.** Two mistakes: (a) turning away and giving up; (b) being so afraid of popping up that you hit into the net instead. Fix: if you pop it up, stay in the point and defend.
8. **Paddle low when moving up to the kitchen.** Move with paddle up and pointed toward the play, so any short blocked ball is immediately reachable.

## Visual Notes

Tyler demonstrates the non-dominant hand touching paddle after each dink (shown in slow motion). The "drop two balls, react" drill shown with Michele — she has to identify forehand or backhand before moving.
