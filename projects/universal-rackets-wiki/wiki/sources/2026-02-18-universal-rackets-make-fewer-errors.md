# How to Make Fewer Errors, Miss Less & Win More (Universal Rackets)

**Source:** YouTube
**Channel:** [Universal Rackets](../entities/universal-rackets.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3vPYoF4efu8
**Duration:** 13:00
**Uploaded:** 2026-02-18

## Key Takeaways

- **"You don't need to hit more winners — you need to make fewer errors."** The pickleball ball is made to come back. At club level, the team that misses less wins. Stop trying to end points; start trying to make opponents hit more balls.
- **Drives: 60-70% power.** The 100% drive looks good but costs 80% of attempts. A 60-70% drive forced Michele to miss during the demo — same result, far more consistent.
- **The math:** An in-ball (even if not great) gives the opponent a chance to miss. A ball hit out is 100% your opponent's point.
- **Drops: play with height — don't try to be perfect.** Being afraid of the pop-up leads to hitting in the net (100% loss). A slightly high drop still gives your opponent a chance to miss. "Over the net's better than in the net." Drill: 10 consecutive drops in the kitchen — height allowed, net not allowed; if it goes in the net, start over.
- **Volleys: placement over power, multiple shots if needed.** Don't try to end the point with one volley. Hit the first with high margin, then adjust. Move the ball to the opponent's backhand, then move them again.
- **Dinks: open the paddle face and push forward.** Same simple cue as the dinking masterclass: push don't hit, open face, go forward.
- **"You live to play another point."** Even a popped-up ball may not be put away. Stay in the point, defend, and get back into position.
- **Placement over power wins every time.** Put the ball where your opponent isn't.
