# Transform Your Pickleball Serve in 10 Minutes (Universal Rackets)

## Key Takeaways

A rapid-fire checklist format (6:38 runtime) — six tips to apply immediately:

1. **Face the net post, not the target.** Right-handed player → align body with the right net post. Facing the target eliminates rotation and forces an arm-only serve.
2. **Follow through and touch your back.** Stopping the swing short forces reliance on gravity and reduces speed. Finish with the paddle touching your upper back, or think "elbow points toward target."
3. **Hips sideways before contact, facing forward after.** Hips = rotation = power. Think: hips sideways at setup, hips facing target at finish. This rotation is the alternative power source beyond arm strength.
4. **Keep body down and forward; don't pick up the head.** A laser from the center of the chest should point toward the target throughout the swing. Picking up the head sends that laser skyward.
5. **Use your legs.** Rock back into the back leg, then explode forward. The legs connect the ground force to the paddle.
6. **Swing low to high; don't be afraid of the pickle ball police.** A low-to-high swing creates topspin and consistency. "Unless you look like Roger Federer at Wimbledon, no one is calling you. Swing freely."
