# CRBN TruFoam Barrage Review (Universal Rackets)

**Source:** YouTube
**Channel:** [Universal Rackets](../entities/universal-rackets.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_XK4-iiD2qY
**Duration:** 21:25
**Uploaded:** 2026-02-27

## Key Takeaways

Tyler and Michele's first impressions and on-court review:

- **The Barrage = CRBN Shape 4 with TruFoam core.** CRBN has four shapes (1, 2, 3, 4). The Barrage adds holes throughout the paddle face (no core crush possible) — a true foam core, 4th generation technology. Marketed as "maximum spin and dwell time" with "carbon fiber reinforced edges."
- **Positioned for power players.** Unlike the TruFoam Waves (which Tyler plays normally — "maximum spin"), the Barrage targets raw power. Tyler describes it as feeling heavier in hand and "like a trampoline." Michele notes it's easier to generate power.
- **14 mm paddle.** Standard modern thickness for balance of power and control.
- **First impressions:** "Feels heavier than Waves but crisp." Michele: "Like the perfect handle size for two-handed backhand." Tyler: "The orange butt cap is my favorite part."
- **CRBN's brand reliability:** 30-day money back guarantee, strong customer service, anti-defect policy cited. Tyler and Michele are sponsored by CRBN, playing the True Foam Waves themselves.
- **Authentication feature:** Can connect a phone to authenticate paddle legitimacy.
- **Comparison to their normal paddle (TruFoam Waves):** Barrage = more power/pop; Waves = more spin. Both share the TruFoam technology.

## Visual Notes

Unboxing shown — the orange butt cap and the holes in the paddle face are distinctly visible. Both test dinking first, then drives.
