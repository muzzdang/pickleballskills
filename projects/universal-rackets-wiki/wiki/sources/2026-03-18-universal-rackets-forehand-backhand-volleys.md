# How to Hit Forehand and Backhand Volleys (Universal Rackets)

**Source:** YouTube
**Channel:** [Universal Rackets](../entities/universal-rackets.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e2EftsV0CrE
**Duration:** 27:16
**Uploaded:** 2026-03-18

## Key Takeaways

- **The volley problem: players don't start in the right position, so they can't get there in time.** Paddle down or close to the body → when ball comes fast, they're late. Fix: paddle out in front and away from the body at ALL TIMES. "Airing out your armpits." "Hugging a tree."
- **80% backhand rule for volleys.** Anything at or across the body toward the left (for righties) is a backhand. "Too many players try to hit forehand when the ball comes toward them." Taking unnecessary forehands from the body center = late, awkward, poppable.
- **Block volley when attacked: squeeze paddle, open face, redirect.** When ball comes fast, no time for swing — squeeze the grip slightly, open the paddle face so the laser points up, and redirect the ball back at the attacker's feet.
- **For high balls: snap the wrist down.** If ball is above net height and you're volleying, the wrist snap replaces the full swing. Elbow in, wrist from high to low — generates pace without a swing arc.
- **For below-net offensive rolls: paddle tip down, finish up.** Same as offensive roll volley from the offensive masterclass — not a downward swing, but an upward brush with the paddle going tip-down to tip-up.
- **The full ready position system.** For dinks: weight on toes, lean forward, hold paddle out front. When someone speeds up: squeeze paddle, hold position.
- **After a speed-up or volley exchange: immediately reset to ready position.** "Where I start is where I finish, where I finish is where I start." This eliminates repositioning time and appears to give faster hands.
