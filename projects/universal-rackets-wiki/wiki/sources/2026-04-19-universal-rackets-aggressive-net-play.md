# Play Aggressive Pickleball at the Net (Universal Rackets)

**Channel:** [Universal Rackets](../entities/universal-rackets.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YZNcH61Xb1o
**Duration:** 12:29 | **Uploaded:** 2026-04-19

## Key Takeaways

- Weight on toes, lean forward — not heels; hovering over kitchen line. "Have toes close to the line." This is the foundation that enables all offensive play.
- Take ball out in front and early — contact at feet = defensive; contact out front = offensive. "Anytime I make contact close to my body, that's defensive or neutral. Anytime I make contact more out in front and away, that's offensive."
- Paddle extended out front always — not just "up." The common failure: paddle is "up" but close to the body, so you still have to reach out to the ball. The paddle must be extended out AND away, making the ball meet the paddle.
- Crab walk side to side; never turn back; hold ground at the kitchen
- Shift laterally to create space for slow balls before attacking
- Get low in legs and push forward — not bend at waist
- Elbow tucked for fast hands in exchanges; limits backswing, keeps paddle compact
- Wrist snap for power on high balls at the net — not full arm swing
- Finish forward after speedups — ready for cleanup, don't fall back
- "The two triggers": ball is high = put it away. Ball is away from your body = you can be aggressive. Both give you offense. Ball close to body = can only block.

## Visual Notes

Tyler demonstrates the extended paddle position — shows how even when the paddle is "up," it can still be too close. Shows the lean-forward position with toes near the line vs. heels-back passive stance. The paddle-extended position shrinks the court for opponents.
