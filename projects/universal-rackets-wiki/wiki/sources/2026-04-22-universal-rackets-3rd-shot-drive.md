# Stop Missing Your 3rd Shot Drive (Universal Rackets)

**Channel:** [Universal Rackets](../entities/universal-rackets.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YemPuRFAiYA
**Duration:** 20:23 | **Uploaded:** 2026-04-22

## Key Takeaways

- **Early prep:** paddle back *before* moving to ball; don't move then prep
- **Turn with paddle away from body** — hug a tree; turn with paddle tight = jammed contact
- **Contact out in front:** same as always; stay turned even when moving up through transition
- **Head down through contact:** pick head up after; lifting early sends ball long
- **60-70% power:** slower drive is more consistent and stays lower — better short reply
- **Goal of the drive:** short reply, not a winner; move forward expecting it
- **Follow your shot:** move toward where ball went, not straight up middle
- **Drive → Drop:** never hit two drives in a row; drop after every drive as you advance
- **Split stop:** stop when opponent is about to contact your drive; advance in stages

## Visual Notes

Tyler demonstrates early prep vs. late prep side by side. Split stop shown in the transition zone context — stopping as the opponent contacts, then advancing again when direction is known.
