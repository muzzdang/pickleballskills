# How to Reset Any Hard Shot (Universal Rackets)

**Channel:** [Universal Rackets](../entities/universal-rackets.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=e6sSNUd2mRc
**Duration:** 16:24 | **Uploaded:** 2026-04-25

## Key Takeaways

- Paddle out in front and away from body; paddle meets the ball, not the ball meets the paddle. "You can't reset a ball if you're hitting it — the goal of the reset is to hold your paddle there."
- Three common reset failures: (1) too big a backswing, (2) pulling paddle away at contact out of fear, (3) wrong paddle face angle (often too open, causing pop-up). All three have one cause: not in the proper position.
- Elbow tucked to eliminate backswing — the most common cause of a big swing. No time in pickleball for a backswing when someone is attacking.
- "Follow the ball with your paddle after you hit." When you hit a shot, point the paddle toward where the ball went — this naturally pre-positions you for the incoming reset shot. "That was a huge change in my game."
- Square (neutral) paddle face for resets — NOT open like for dinks; open face = pop-up
- Don't flinch; get in athletic low stance so you absorb pace rather than getting pushed
- Redirect bangers with a low return — forces them to hit up, stopping the attack
- Progressive drill: start at 20% pace, build to 100%

## Visual Notes

Tyler and Michele do the progressive pace drill. Tyler demonstrates the "follow your shot with paddle" concept — showing how after a dink, if the paddle tracks the dink direction, it's already halfway to the reset position. The three failure modes are shown: big backswing, flinch-away, and wrong face angle.
