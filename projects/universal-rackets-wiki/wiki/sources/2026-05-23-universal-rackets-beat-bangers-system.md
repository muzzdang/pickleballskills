# How to Beat Bangers in Pickleball — The Complete System

**Uploaded:** 2026-05-23

## Key Takeaways

- **Wide stance + hold ground**: feet hip-width, drop center of gravity, stay forward. The moment you back up or flinch, you hand the banger the point — you pop up, they bang again.
- **Block, don't swing**: absorb pace and redirect; no backswing whatsoever. Paddle face slightly open (upward) so the block clears the net without popping up. "Have the paddle meet the ball, not the ball meet the paddle."
- **Aim at their feet**: keeps the ball low (bangers can only bang from height), handcuffs them at contact (no space = no power), prevents them from advancing to the kitchen.
- **Two-handed block (Tyler's signature tip)**: place the non-dominant hand on the paddle grip for stability. Eliminates flinching and flailing; creates a wall instead of a one-handed redirect. Tyler credits this with solving his own banger problem against a former ATP-100 player.
- **Psychological game**: show them it doesn't work, then let balls go out. Bangers only bang because it works — prove it doesn't and they self-destruct (hit harder → error → lose confidence).

## Top 3 Kitchen Mistakes vs Bangers

1. Trying to out-bang them
2. Panicking and moving back
3. Popping the shot up

All three give the banger the high ball they need. Eliminating them alone wins most exchanges.

## Drill Progression

Partner starts throwing balls, then hitting at increasing pace. Goal: get comfortable seeing speed before full-pace blocking. "Like a baseball player — the faster the pitching, the more you need reps to see it at speed."
