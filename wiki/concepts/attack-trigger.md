# Attack Trigger

The single rule for when to hit hard: **only when contact is above the net**. Every other ball — place it.

## Why this works

A ball above the net height means you can hit down into the court with power and still land it in bounds. A ball below the net forces you to hit up to clear it, which means your opponent can attack anything you send back.

Trying to hit hard on a low ball almost always results in either an error or a ball your opponent can put away.

## In practice

- Working a dinking rally? Keep placing until you get a high ball — then attack
- Opponent hits a great drop that keeps you back? Reset low, don't try to drive it
- Tempted to speed up from the kitchen on a low ball? Don't — it's [placement over power](placement-over-power.md) territory

## The discipline

Implementing this feels unnatural for players who come from power sports (tennis, racquetball). The instinct is to go for it. The discipline is recognizing that you haven't earned the right to attack yet — and doing the work to earn it through [side-to-side dinking](side-to-side-dinking.md) or [transition zone](transition-zone.md) pressure first.
