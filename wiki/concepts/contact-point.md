# Contact Point

Where in space you make contact with the ball relative to your body. For powerful groundstrokes and drives, the ideal contact point is **out in front and away from the body**, roughly in line with the front knee — far enough to have a full arm extension through the swing.

## Why spacing matters

When the ball is too close to the body, the arm is cramped and can't generate force. Extended contact gives the arm room to accelerate through the ball.

## Getting into position

- Stay on the **balls of your feet** with small, active shuffle steps
- As the ball approaches, **push your arm out** to create space and get the ball to the front-knee contact zone
- Avoid planting feet and reaching — use footwork to put your body in the right place

## Where it applies most

- Return of serve (time to set up)
- Baseline drives
- Less applicable mid-court (less time, less need for maximum power)

## On resets from transition

When resetting a ball in the [transition zone](transition-zone.md), put your paddle down and make contact out in front of your body. Same principle — extended contact gives you control and prevents popping the ball up.
