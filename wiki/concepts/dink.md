# Dink

A soft shot hit at or near the kitchen line designed to arc gently over the net and land in the opponent's Non-Volley Zone (NVZ / kitchen). The dink is the central exchange of high-level pickleball — the game's equivalent of the baseline rally in tennis.

## Purpose

The dink's goal is to **deny the opponent an attackable ball** while creating opportunities for your own attack. A perfect dink lands low in the kitchen, forcing the opponent to hit up — and any ball hit upward can be attacked. The dink is not passive; it is controlled pressure.

## Mechanics: Kieran's Framework (Dinkcredibles / PPB)

### Foundational Drills
1. **Dink tosses:** Partner tosses balls softly while you practice the contact and follow-through without a paddle. Trains the arm path in isolation.
2. **Paddle half-hits:** Use only the top half of the paddle face. Forces centered contact and prevents edge hits.
3. **Scoop and catch:** Swing through the dink motion and catch the ball in the non-dominant hand to feel the correct finish position.

### Body Cues
- **Legs = springs:** Legs absorb and generate energy. The drop into the dink loads the legs; the return to standing drives the swing.
- **Arm = ribbon:** The arm stays loose and "ribbony" through contact. Tension kills dink consistency. Tense grip → shanks.
- **Semi-open stance:** Feet at 45 degrees to the net. This loads the hips for rotation and prevents arm-only mechanics.
- **Hip swivel:** The dink should begin with a gentle hip rotation — not the arm reaching across the body. Hip drives, arm follows.
- **Tripod foot:** Weight distributed across three points — heel, ball of foot under big toe, ball of foot under pinky toe. Activates the glutes, stabilizes the ankle-knee chain.

## Two-Handed Backhand Dink (Luke Larsen / PPB)

The two-handed backhand dink (or roll dink) is emerging as a high-level offensive dink option:
- Continental grip on the paddle hand
- Light grip pressure throughout — "broken wrist" feel
- Contact inside the left knee (for righties) in a "V-stance"
- Low-to-high shoulder-driven swing, not arm-push
- Follow the ball for 6–8 inches after contact to prevent the flick/long error

See: [Two-Handed Backhand](two-handed-backhand.md), [Roll Shot](roll-shot.md)

## Backhand Dink Mechanics: Body Fix (Kieran / PPB)

Common cause of the backhand dink yip is a body mechanics failure:
- Adductor tightness → narrow stance → hip instability
- Collapsed arch (foot rolling inward) → knee caves → hip destabilizes → arm path becomes inconsistent
- Fix: tripod foot loading, outside leg stabilization, knee tracking over toe

## Cooperative Drilling Progression

Per Kieran (Dinkcredibles), dink drilling should follow the cooperative-to-competitive ladder:
1. **Cooperative skinny singles:** 100 consecutive dinks crosscourt on half-court before any competitive format
2. **Cooperative-competitive:** One feeds, one competes (5 transition target)
3. **Dink game to 3:** Short competitive sets between drilling blocks
