# Drilling With Intention

## Definition

A practice philosophy that prioritizes quality, match-relevant repetitions over casual cooperative hitting. The goal is every rep at match speed and match quality — not just keeping the ball in play.

## Core Principles

- **Match-quality reps only**: lazy dinks that never miss are worthless; if you're not moving your feet, pressuring your partner, and getting out of breath, you're not drilling — you're just hitting
- **Specificity**: drill the exact situation you want to improve; don't mix randomly; come to a session with 1–2 things to work on
- **Progressive stages**: cooperative → semi-competitive → competitive; start cooperative to learn the motion, then add context/pressure; don't go straight to competitive for a new skill
- **Both partners benefit**: communicate what each person needs for the session; alternate being "selfish" so each player gets focused reps on their own priorities
- **Timed vs. scored**: Trang Quinn's approach — set a timer and focus on patterns/quality, not the score; this keeps attention on execution rather than results

## Example Sequences

### Mari Humberg's Dink Sequence
(See full detail in [source](../sources/2026-05-22-mari-humberg-drilling-dinks.md))
1. 100 dinks in a row down the line (left side)
2. 100 dinks in a row down the line (right side)
3. 100 dinks crosscourt (both sides)
4. Dink game to 5/11 — no attacks allowed regardless of ball height; try to win with placement, rolling, and pressure only
- Add consequences for misses (busts up, lives system) to simulate competitive pressure

### Trang Quinn's Full Session Order
1. Straight dinking
2. Crosscourt dinking
3. Reset game (get ball to bounce in kitchen 5 times while partner tries to prevent it)
4. Drops and drives from baseline
5. Cooperative volleys
6. Speed-up game (alternating who initiates)
7. Combo pattern drill

### Pickleball Playbook's 4-Drill Consistency Sequence
1. Drop Consistency Game (game-to-11 format, dropper scores from baseline)
2. Reset Consistency Game (same format, stuck in transition zone)
3. Volley Consistency Drill (both at midcourt, cooperative to 50)
4. Dink Shallow Game (cones mark zone; take out of air to score bonus point)

### Briones 3-Ball Counter Drill
Feeder sends balls to dominant side, body, and non-dominant side in random order — receiver must counter all three without being told which comes next; builds neutral paddle position and reaction across all body zones

## Learning Order (Pickleball Playbook)

Control → Consistency → Location → Power

Add power last. Players who chase power before consistency get inconsistent; players who master control first find power emerges naturally from good mechanics.
