# Drilling

Deliberate, structured practice of specific skills in isolation — as opposed to game play, where all skills are blended and pressure prevents focused repetition. Drilling is the primary mechanism for breaking through rating plateaus.

## Why Drilling Beats Game Play for Improvement

Game play reinforces existing habits. Drilling builds new ones. Austin Hardy ([Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)) identifies "playing only games instead of drilling" as the single most common cause of the 3.5–4.0 plateau. His recommended ratio for stuck players: **60% drilling / 40% game play.**

## Cooperative-to-Competitive Progression (Kieran / Dinkcredibles)

Kieran's core methodology for structuring drilling sessions:

1. **Fully cooperative:** Both players help each other succeed. No competitive element. Target: 100 consecutive dinks (skinny singles), or a set number of clean transitions (5 transition shots). This builds the neural pattern without pressure interference.
2. **Cooperative-competitive:** One player feeds cooperatively; the other executes with competitive intent. A specific success target (5 clean transitions, 3 put-aways) before switching roles.
3. **Fully competitive:** Normal game play (to 3 or to 11). The competitive format transfers skills built in isolation into game situations. Skipping directly to competition before cooperative phases causes players to default to bad habits under pressure.

## Five-Part Training Routine (Austin Hardy / PPB)

Austin credits this routine for his 4.0→5.0 rating jump in 3 months:
1. **Dynamic warm-up** — footwork without paddle (leg swings, lateral shuffles)
2. **Isolated technical drill** — one shot, one mechanic, 15–20 minutes
3. **Pattern drill** — 2–3 shot sequences
4. **Competitive points with defined focus** — not random play; a specific tactical objective
5. **Film review or coaching debrief**

## Tournament Preparation (Kieran / Dinkcredibles)

Pre-tournament schedule:
- **Night before:** Low-intensity session — light ball touching, no hard drilling. Preserve muscles, don't disrupt sleep.
- **Day of (on-court warm-up):** Dynamic movement → soft dinking → transition shots → full-pace drives and serves. Always graduated, never cold.
- **Off-court daily:** Shadow footwork with paddle in front of a mirror, 5 minutes daily. Builds split-step, box step, and kitchen approach patterns without a court.

## Specific Drills by Category

**Dink drills (Kieran):**
- Dink tosses — practice arm path without paddle
- Paddle half-hits — top half of face only; forces centered contact
- Scoop and catch — swing through and catch the ball to feel finish position
- Skinny singles cooperative — 100 consecutive dinks crosscourt on half court
- Dink game to 3 — short competitive sets between drilling blocks

**Hands-battle drills (Austin Hardy):**
- Double-hit volleys — two hits before passing to partner
- Romanian volleys — volley while moving laterally with open base
- Hills up — start close, step back after each success; return to close

**Transition drills:**
- Cooperative-competitive transition — 5 clean baseline-to-kitchen transitions before going competitive
- MFWC shadow drilling — practice the Monitor-Flow-Watch-Crash decision sequence without a ball
