# Drive

A hard, flat or topspin shot hit with pace — the opposite of a drop. In pickleball, the drive is most commonly discussed in the context of the third shot (serving team's second shot) and the "drip" hybrid.

## The Austin Hardy Reframe: Drive = Defensive Setup

[Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md) (Austin Hardy) offers a counterintuitive but important reframe of the conventional drive-vs-drop framing:

- **Drive = defensive setup.** A third-shot drive gives the return team time to react, volley the ball back, and maintain their kitchen position. The serving team remains at or near the baseline, defending.
- **Drop = offensive move.** A drop advances the serving team to the kitchen — an offensive position. The drop is not a defensive shot; it is a tactical advance.

This flips the common beginner assumption that driving is aggressive and dropping is passive.

## When to Drive (Decision Framework)

Use the drive when:
- The opponent's return is above the net tape (attackable height)
- You have enough time and position to generate clean pace
- The opponent is in the transition zone — a drive at a moving target is harder to handle than a stationary one
- You want to deliberately create a pop-up for a follow-up drop or attack

Avoid driving when:
- Ball is below the net tape (drive sends it into the net)
- You are reaching or off-balance
- Opponent is already established at the kitchen (they will volley it back easily)

## The "Drip" Hybrid

Austin Hardy introduced the "drip" (drive + drop hybrid):
- Execute a drive swing with a drop trajectory — the pace of a drive but the arc of a drop
- Forces opponents to decide late whether to volley or let it bounce
- Higher success rate than either a pure drive (which may pop up) or a pure drop (which may be predictable)
- Used as a default when the drive-or-drop decision is unclear

## Drive Depth Rule

[Ben Johns](../entities/ben-johns.md)'s Rule 6: keep depth on drives. A short drive sits up in the opponent's wheelhouse. A deep drive pushes them back and compresses their decision time.
