# DUPR

Dynamic Universal Pickleball Rating — the most widely used skill rating system in pickleball. Ratings are calculated from match results across all formats (recreational, tournament, leagues) and update dynamically after each game.

## Rating scale (approximate)

| Rating | Level |
|--------|-------|
| 2.0–3.0 | Beginner |
| 3.0–3.5 | Novice |
| 3.5–4.0 | Intermediate |
| 4.0–4.5 | Advanced intermediate |
| 4.5–5.0 | Advanced |
| 5.0+ | Elite / pro |

## The 5.0 ceiling

5.0 is a meaningful benchmark — it's the entry point for elite amateur and low-pro play. Getting from 4.5 to 5.0 is notoriously difficult; ratings in the 4.7–4.9 range can plateau for months or years despite consistent tournament play. [Pickleball Studio](../entities/pickleball-studio.md)'s Chris peaked at 4.78 after a full tournament season and dropped back to 4.71.

## DUPR vs other ratings

DUPR has largely supplanted the older USAPA self-rating system for competitive play. Tournament brackets are increasingly organized by DUPR range rather than self-reported skill level.

## Known Problems (Austin Hardy / PPB Critique)

Austin Hardy (Pickleball Playbook) raised a detailed critique of DUPR in April 2026 based on his personal experience:

- **Personal example:** Austin won a 5.0-rated tournament with a 5.3 DUPR — and his rating went **DOWN** to 5.2 afterward. Winning a below-rated bracket penalized him.
- **Combined ratings flaw:** DUPR doubles ratings average partner ratings. Two 4.0 players = same combined rating as a 5.0 + a 3.0 — but the 4.0/4.0 pair will consistently beat the 5.0/3.0 pair. The equivalence is false.
- **Score differential distortion:** DUPR weights score margins in its algorithm, incentivizing running up scores against weaker opponents and creating perverse tournament incentives.
- **Wrong bracket problem:** Rating technicalities can place higher-rated players in lower brackets, and winning those brackets actively hurts their rating.
- **Alternative in development:** Austin announced he is building an alternative system based on win/loss only (no score differential), partner-independent individual ratings, and bracket-quality normalization. No launch date announced.
