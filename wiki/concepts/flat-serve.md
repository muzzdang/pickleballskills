# Flat Serve

The flat serve (also called the fast serve or power serve) is a low-trajectory, high-pace serve with minimal spin. It is one of three primary serve types alongside the [topspin serve](serve-mechanics.md) and the short angled serve.

## Characteristics

- **Low trajectory:** the ball travels nearly level over the net, leaving little margin for error
- **High pace:** the flat swing path transfers more energy into forward velocity than into spin
- **Minimal spin:** the ball bounces predictably on the receiver's side — unlike topspin, which bounces high and aggressively

## When to Use

- As a **change-of-pace** serve after establishing a topspin serve — the receiver is primed for a spinning ball and a flat one throws off timing
- When **targeting wide angles** — flat pace reaches the sideline faster, giving less time to react
- When targeting the **body** — pace into the body limits the receiver's swing and produces a weak return

## Technique

The key difference from the topspin serve is the contact zone:
- **Contact point is higher** — driving through the equator of the ball rather than brushing underneath
- **Swing path is more level** — not low-to-high; roughly horizontal through contact
- **Less wrist snap** — the wrist still loads ([wrist lag](wrist-lag.md)) but the release is more forward than upward

## Trade-offs vs. Topspin

| | Flat Serve | Topspin Serve |
|---|---|---|
| Margin for error | Lower | Higher |
| Bounce behavior | Predictable/low | High, aggressive |
| Receiver reaction time | Less | More |
| Consistency | More difficult | Easier |

## Common Error

Trying to hit a flat serve at 100% effort causes the most faults. The flat serve's margin is already narrow — reduce pace to 80-85% and focus on placement rather than maximum velocity.
