# Fourth Shot

## What It Is

The returning team's second shot — hit after the serving team's third shot lands. It's the shot that determines whether the returning team **keeps** their offensive advantage or gives it away.

The return of serve creates the advantage (returning team gets to advance while servers must let the ball bounce). The 4th shot is how that advantage is preserved or surrendered.

## Three Goals (in priority order)

1. **Keep opponents back** — prevent servers from reaching the kitchen
2. **Apply downward pressure** — find their feet; anything going down must come up, giving you more attackable balls
3. **Avoid attackable balls** — don't give them a high contact point; this dictates whether you take the ball out of the air or step back

## The Four Options

### 1. Roll Volley
**When:** 3rd shot is mid-level or higher and you've reached the NVZ.
**How:** Take it out of the air; brush upward with topspin; aim down at their feet.
**Why:** Takes time away from opponents; topspin arc brings ball down at their feet.
- Contact well in front of body, at or slightly below net height
- Weight forward, leaning into the kitchen
- See: [Roll Shot](roll-shot.md)

### 2. Strong Block
**When:** 3rd shot is a drive coming at you.
**How:** Split-step to stop momentum, meet the ball out front with a firm "wall" — elbow ~90°, knuckles forward, wrist locked — and redirect deep at their feet.
**Key distinction from reset block:** This is not a soft absorb. It's meeting force with equal-and-opposite force. Weight even to slightly forward, not backing off.
- Backhand: keep elbow strong, knuckles forward, "stick the landing"
- Forehand: elbow in, compact, same wall concept
- The ball's speed returns it deep without needing to swing

### 3. Step Back and Drive
**When:** 3rd is too low to take cleanly out of the air; taking it at its highest point isn't possible.
**How:** Recognize early, step back off the ball, turn shoulders fully ("open the door / close the door"), drive off the bounce at their feet, then re-advance.
**The most underused and misunderstood option.** Players over-commit to the NVZ and take the ball too low, forcing a ball that pops up.
- Full shoulder turn into the back leg is the cue (visual: "TURN YOUR BODY / TURN YOUR SHOULDERS")
- Drive rolls down to feet — not a flat drive into the body
- See: [Third Shot Drive](third-shot-drive.md) for the mechanics (same motion)

### 4. Dink and Neutralize
**When:** 3rd is a great shot — you're reaching, ball is well below the net, no aggressive option exists.
**How:** Soften the hand, dink into the kitchen, concede the kitchen line temporarily, reset the point from neutral.
**This is the safety valve.** Trying to force an aggressive 4th from a bad position is the error.
- Short-hopping a fast low ball leads to a ball that pops up — exactly what you're trying to avoid
- Accept neutral; play from the kitchen
- See: [Dink Mechanics](dink-mechanics.md)

## Decision Framework (simple if/then)

| Incoming 3rd shot | 4th shot choice |
|---|---|
| Mid-level or higher, at NVZ | Roll volley |
| Driven at you | Strong block |
| Can't take at highest point out of air | Step back and drive |
| Reaching / well below net | Dink and neutralize |

## Most Common Mistake

Over-committing to the NVZ and taking the ball at the low point out of the air. The only option from below the net is to hit up — into the waiting attacks of the opponents. **Recognizing when to step back is the key skill.**

## When You're Already at the NVZ (4th Shot from Kitchen)

If the returning team has reached the NVZ, the 4th shot becomes a **contact height decision** — which shot to use based on where the ball is when it reaches you. Take every ball out of the air when possible (earlier contact = more offensive position, takes time away).

**Forehand:** High → swinging volley; Net level → roll (low-to-high); Below knee → step back, attack off bounce.

**Backhand:** High → swinging volley; Net level → backhand flick (topspin + pace); Slightly below net/above knee → backhand roll; Below knee → step back, 2H backhand off bounce.

**Rule:** Never volley below knee level. Create space, gather feet, attack off the bounce at the apex.

See: [Kitchen Aggression](kitchen-aggression.md) for the full height-based framework and drive-drop counter scenarios.
