# Grip Position

Where on the handle you hold the paddle. Lower = more power; higher (choked up) = faster hands.

## The physics

The further the paddle head is from the pivot point (your grip), the faster it moves through the swing — like the tip of a whip. Gripping low extends this distance, increasing paddle head speed and therefore power. It also gives more reach on wide balls.

## Guidelines by situation

| Situation | Grip | Why |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Serving, baseline drives | Low (pinky on bottom edge or below) | Maximum power and reach |
| Mid-court | Neutral | Balance of power and control |
| Kitchen / dinking | Choked up slightly | Faster hands for quick exchanges |

## Common mistake

Gripping near the throat at the kitchen when you need fast hands — rare at the pro level except during dink exchanges.
