# Grip Pressure

How tightly you hold the paddle. Counterintuitively, a **loose grip during the swing** followed by a **firm snap at contact** generates more power than gripping tightly throughout.

## The whip concept

A stiff grip locks the paddle face and prevents it from accelerating at the moment of contact. A loose-then-snap motion lets the paddle head "whip" through impact, adding a final burst of velocity just as the ball is struck.

## Execution

1. Hold loosely through the backswing and into the forward swing
2. At the moment of contact, stiffen the grip — the paddle face snaps closed
3. The snap transfers the final energy into the ball

## Who should use this

**Intermediate to advanced players only.** The wrist action adds variability to every shot. Beginners should focus on consistent contact first; add this once the stroke is grooved.

Also applies to the [roll shot](roll-shot.md) at the kitchen and to serves (wrist lag then snap).

## Common mistake

Death-gripping throughout the rally — especially under pressure. Results in a stiff, powerless swing with less control than a relaxed stroke.
