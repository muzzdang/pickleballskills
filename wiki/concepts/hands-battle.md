# Hands Battle

A rapid-fire exchange of volleys at the kitchen line where both teams are trading hard, flat shots at close range. Hands battles are the most physically demanding and technically challenging sequences in pickleball — they are won through preparation and positioning, not raw reaction speed.

## The System (Austin Hardy / PPB — FAST Framework)

Austin Hardy distills hands-battle success into the **FAST** framework:

- **F — Fully Ready:** Reset the paddle to ready position after every single shot. Players who let the paddle drift after contact are already late on the next ball.
- **A — Aim:** Pick a target before the ball arrives. Don't decide in the air — have a plan (body, middle, open court) before contact.
- **S — Shift:** Use the [box step](footwork.md) to position the body behind the ball. Reach-and-swing is always less accurate and less powerful than a positioned contact.
- **T — Train:** Hands-battle speed cannot be built through game play alone. Dedicated drilling (double-hit volleys, Romanian volleys, Hills up volleys) is required.

## Drills for Hand Speed (Austin Hardy / PPB)

Three progressions:

1. **Double-hit volleys:** Players alternate hitting two volleys before passing to partner. Trains paddle recovery and rhythm.
2. **Romanian volleys:** Players volley at half-speed while moving laterally (open base). Training hands exchanges while feet are not set makes standing hands battles feel easy.
3. **Hills up volleys:** Start 1 foot from partner. After each successful exchange, one step back. Reach maximum distance, then return. Simulates varying pace and changing angles.

## Body Mechanics: The Hayden Patriquin Model

[Hayden Patriquin](../entities/hayden-patriquin.md) is cited by Austin Hardy as the elite benchmark for hands-battle mechanics:

- **Elbows out and away:** Never tucked against the body. Out elbows create a wider coverage zone.
- **Sandwich position (FH):** On forehand volleys, paddle face closes slightly with elbow driving through contact — like sandwiching the ball between the paddle and an imaginary wall.
- **Eagle position (BH):** On backhand volleys, the arm extends outward like a wing — elbow leads, arm away from body, contact out in front.

## Attack Targets

- **Body shots win hands battles:** Balls directed at the opponent's hip or shoulder are the hardest to deflect cleanly. [Ben Johns](../entities/ben-johns.md) and Hayden Patriquin both use this deliberately — attacking the body rather than chasing open court.
- **Middle seam:** Balls directed at the seam between partners force a communication decision and reduce the opponent's angle options.

## Defense in Hands Battles

- **Give space:** When attacked hard, absorb slightly backward rather than pushing into the ball. Creating space allows the paddle to move without full-arm compensation.
- **Choke up:** Slide the grip hand up the handle 1–2 inches at the kitchen. Shorter lever = faster hand exchanges.
- **Wide base:** Feet wider than shoulder-width. Never narrow or crossed at the kitchen during a hands battle.
