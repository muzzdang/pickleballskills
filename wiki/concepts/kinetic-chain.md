# Kinetic Chain

The sequence of body segments that transfer power from the ground up into the ball: **legs → hips → core → arm → paddle**. Using the full chain multiplies power far beyond what the arm alone can generate, and is easier to control because the arm doesn't have to over-swing.

## Execution

1. **Load** — as the ball approaches, transfer weight to the back foot (you should be able to lift the front foot)
2. **Drive** — plant the front foot firmly before contact
3. **Snap** — close the hips so the chest faces the ball at the moment of contact, then faces forward as the stroke finishes
4. **Transfer** — the hip snap carries all the stored energy through the arm and into the paddle

The key signal that it's working: your chest is facing toward the ball at contact, then rotates through to face the net on the follow-through.

## Common mistake

Using only the arm — generates less power and requires a bigger, harder-to-control arm swing.
