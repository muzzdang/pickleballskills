# Laser Pointer Theory

A mental model for ready position: imagine a laser projecting from the tip of your paddle. Keep that laser pointed at the ball at all times when the ball is on your opponent's side of the court.

## Why it works

When the paddle points at the ball, it simultaneously:
- Keeps the paddle in your field of vision (better proprioception)
- Aligns your hips to face the ball
- Positions your paddle to respond to any direction

When your paddle is behind your field of vision, your brain estimates its location rather than knowing it — leading to off-center contact and misread volleys.

## The backhand shade

Don't point the laser *directly* at the ball. For right-handed players, point slightly to the **left** of the ball to shade toward the backhand. This is because the backhand covers more of your body than the forehand does. By shading left, you're always pre-loaded to respond to the most common direction of attack.

Left-handed players: shade slightly right.

## Applies everywhere

- At the kitchen: paddle up, laser on ball
- In transition: lower paddle, laser on ball
- At the baseline: low ready position, laser on ball

Combined with the [seesaw effect](seesaw-effect.md) (paddle height adjusting by zone), the laser pointer creates an always-optimal ready position wherever you are on court.
