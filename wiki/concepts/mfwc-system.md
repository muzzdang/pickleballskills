# MFWC System

Austin Hardy's ([Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)) four-step decision framework for every transition shot and kitchen approach. Designed to replace reactive, emotional shot selection with a repeatable process.

## The Four Steps

1. **Monitor** — Read what is coming. Before the ball arrives, assess: Is the ball fast or slow? High or low? Am I in position or reaching? This read happens before contact, not during.
2. **Flow / Watch** — Track the ball from your opponent's paddle all the way to yours. Don't watch where you want to hit it — watch it arrive. "Flow" also refers to moving into position based on what you monitored.
3. **Watch** (some formulations use this as a separate step for opponent tracking) — After you hit, track the ball to where your opponent contacts it. Know what they're doing before committing your next movement.
4. **Crash** — Move toward the kitchen aggressively. Crash is the physical execution step: once Monitor tells you the ball is droppable or attackable, commit the body forward.

## Shot Selection Integration

The Monitor step feeds directly into shot selection:
- Ball is slow, high, attackable → Crash and drive/attack
- Ball is fast, low, difficult → Crash slowly or hold, execute a drop/reset
- Ball is mid-level, uncertain → Default to the drop (conservative)

## Application in the Transition Zone

The MFWC framework is specifically designed for the "no man's land" problem — players caught mid-court guessing whether to attack or reset. By running through the steps on every transition ball, the guess is eliminated.

Austin simplifies it further for mid-court situations as **MWC** (Monitor-Watch-Crash) — the core three-step version used in the "Don't Rush the Kitchen" video with guest coach Mini.

## Common Errors

- **Skipping Monitor:** Reacting before reading the ball type leads to attacking unpattable balls or resetting attackable ones.
- **Crashing without permission:** Moving forward before executing a quality shot. Crash is the reward for a good Monitor read, not the automatic response to hitting any shot.
- **Fear-crashing:** Stopping mid-transition because of uncertainty. Once you commit to crashing, commit fully — hesitation mid-transition is worse than either staying back or going all the way in.
