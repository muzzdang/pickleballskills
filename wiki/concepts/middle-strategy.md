# Middle Strategy

One of the highest-percentage strategies in doubles pickleball: targeting the seam between the two opponents (the center of the court) rather than the sidelines. The middle is simultaneously the best offensive target and the most important defensive priority.

## Why the Middle Works (Offense)

1. **Communication breakdown:** A ball in the middle forces a split-second decision between partners — who takes it? Even a fraction of a second of uncertainty is enough to produce a weak return or an outright miss.
2. **No angle:** Both sideline shots create angles; the middle removes angles. A middle ball is harder to redirect sharply because both players must move toward center to cover it.
3. **Miss margin:** A mis-hit to the sideline goes out. A mis-hit to the middle stays in. The middle is a larger, more forgiving target.
4. **Body exposure:** A middle ball travels toward the body of one or both opponents — body balls are the hardest to return with pace or placement.

## Why You Must Cover the Middle (Defense)

[Ben Johns](../entities/ben-johns.md)'s Rule 1 and Rule 2 both relate to the middle: always cover it; when in doubt, move toward it rather than the sideline. Austin Hardy observes that Ben and [Anna Leigh Waters](../entities/annalie-waters.md) default to middle coverage over sideline coverage on every close positioning decision.

The middle is the highest-probability target for opponents. An uncovered middle concedes the easiest attacking lane.

## Hayden Patriquin vs. Ben Johns

Austin Hardy's breakdown: Hayden Patriquin and Anna Bright defeated Ben Johns and Anna Leigh Waters in a notable match by hitting every single ball up the middle — eliminating Ben's inside-out forehand angles and exposing partner communication uncertainty. This was described as the tactically correct game plan against the world's top pair.

[Hayden Patriquin](../entities/hayden-patriquin.md) also takes middle balls with his forehand — even balls that appear to be his partner's ball. His forehand is strong enough that the poach is a net positive.

## Put-Away Through the Middle

Ben Johns's Rule 5 ([5 Rules Ben Johns & Anna Leigh Use to Always Win](../sources/2026-04-06-gBGRF6IzKQg-5-rules-ben-johns-anna-leigh.md)): put-away shots go through the middle, not the sideline. The sideline is low-margin; the middle is the highest-percentage finish.
