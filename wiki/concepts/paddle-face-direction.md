# Paddle Face Direction

The ball goes where the paddle face points at the moment of contact. This sounds obvious but is one of the most overlooked control variables.

## The rule

Paddle face open (tilted back) → ball goes up (pop-up risk)
Paddle face closed (tilted forward) → ball goes down (net risk)
Paddle face neutral/square → ball goes straight

## Grip pressure and face angle

- **Tight grip** → tends to close the face → balls go lower/into net
- **Loose grip** → tends to open the face → balls go higher

This means [grip pressure](grip-pressure.md) indirectly controls face angle.

## On the backhand dink

Pro players **lock the wrist** on backhand dinks to prevent the face from opening or closing mid-stroke. The locked wrist ensures the face stays pointed exactly where they intend — crosscourt or down the line.

## On the forehand dink

The opposite: **loosen the wrist slightly** on forehand dinks. A locked forehand grip tends to send the ball too wide or too high. Loosening lets the face naturally settle at the correct angle toward the target.

## Aiming with the face

Before hitting, consciously aim the paddle face at the target — not just look at where you want the ball to go. Looking at the target without aligning the face leads to off-axis hits.
