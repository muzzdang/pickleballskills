# Paddle Weight Customization

Adding weight to a pickleball paddle changes its swing weight, twist weight, plow through, and sweet spot size. Most performance paddles benefit from at least some customization.

## Key metrics

**Swing weight** — how hard the paddle is to swing. Lower = more maneuverable; higher = more plow through and stability. Typical range: 105–120+.
- Under 100: ultra-light/maneuverable, sacrifices plow through
- 105–111: easy to swing
- 112–117: average
- 118+: heavier, more sluggish

**Twist weight** — resistance to rotation on off-center hits. Higher = larger effective sweet spot and more forgiveness. Aim for 6.5+ for a forgiving paddle.

**Plow through** — the paddle's resistance to deflection when struck by a hard ball. Low plow through = sweet spot feels small, paddle gets pushed around on hard shots.

## Where to add weight

Pickleball Studio tested 300+ weight configurations across widebody, hybrid, and elongated paddles. Key data:

| Location | Swing Weight Impact | Twist Weight Impact | Best For |
|----------|--------------------|--------------------|---------|
| Bottom corners | Low (+1.3–4.4 per config) | Good (+0.9–1.5) | Most players — best efficiency |
| Sides (3 & 9 o'clock) | Moderate | High | Sweet spot expansion |
| Head (12 o'clock) | Very high (+7–14 per 6–12g) | Low | Power/plow through at cost of hand speed |
| Throat/neck | Very low | Moderate | Stability without swing weight penalty |

**Best configuration for most players:** Bottom corners with 3–5 inches of 1g tape. Gives +1–2 swing weight with +0.9–1.1 twist weight — optimal twist/swing ratio of any setup tested.

**Head weight is a multiplier — use sparingly:**
- 6g at head → +7.4 swing weight
- 12g at head → +14 swing weight
- Even small amounts dramatically hurt hand speed

## Shape-specific guidance

- **Widebody:** Already has high twist weight naturally; can tolerate and benefit from higher swing weight — placing weight higher (sides, upper corners) is acceptable for ball plow-through
- **Elongated:** Already has high swing weight; stick to bottom corners to maximize twist weight without further increasing swing weight
- **Hybrid:** Follow elongated guidance; test bottom corners first

## Typical setup

Start at bottom corners, 3–8g total. A 10-point swing weight change is typically perceptible to most players. For elongated paddles that feel too light, more weight is often appropriate.

## Gen 3 paddles and weight

Many Gen 3 (polypropylene core) paddles benefit significantly from added weight — the stock swing weight is often too low for optimal plow through. The [Franklin Aurelius](../entities/franklin-aurelius.md) at 92 swing weight stock is a good example; it needs weight to reach its performance potential.
