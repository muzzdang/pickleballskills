# Pickleball Rules

Official rules governing pickleball as administered by USA Pickleball (USAP). Rules are updated annually; the 2026 rulebook introduced several notable changes.

## 2026 Rule Changes (Highlighted by Austin Hardy / PPB)

### Net Post Clarification
A serve that contacts the net post and lands in the correct service court is now **in play** — not a fault. Previously, net post contact was always a fault regardless of where the ball landed. This aligns pickleball with some other racket sports.

### Act of Volleying Timing
The "act of volleying" (which triggers NVZ/kitchen violations) now formally begins when a player first moves toward the ball with the intention to volley — not at the moment of contact. This resolves ambiguity about players who start moving into the NVZ before contact.

### Rally Scoring (Pilot)
USA Pickleball continues to pilot rally scoring at select events. Under rally scoring:
- Every rally awards a point, regardless of who served
- Games go to 21, win by 2
- Not yet adopted universally as of 2026

### Serve Motion (Tightened Enforcement)
- The arm must move in an upward arc at contact
- The highest point of the paddle head must not be above the wrist at contact
- Spin serves via finger manipulation before contact remain banned
- Enforcement is stricter — referees are more actively calling serve violations at tournaments

### Extra Ball / Timeout Rules
- A timeout may be called before the score is announced
- Once the server announces the score, the rally is live — no timeouts
- Using an extra ball from a pocket during play is a fault

## Core Rules (Permanent)

**Non-Volley Zone (Kitchen):**
- Players may not volley (hit the ball before it bounces) while standing in the kitchen or touching the kitchen line
- Any momentum carrying a player into the kitchen after a volley is a violation
- Players may enter the kitchen after the ball has bounced

**Double Bounce Rule:**
- The serve must bounce before the return team can hit it
- The return must also bounce before the serving team can hit it
- After two bounces, volleys are permitted

**Serving:**
- Serve is underhand with upward arc
- Contact below waist level
- Paddle head below wrist at contact
- No finger-spin serves
