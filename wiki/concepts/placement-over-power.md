# Placement Over Power

The foundational strategic principle: placing the ball precisely beats hitting it hard, at every level. Most players don't lose because their opponent outplays them — they give away points by forcing winners instead of creating opportunities.

## The mindset shift

- **Wrong:** Try to hit through opponents, speed up balls, go for winners
- **Right:** Move opponents, keep the ball low, wait for a high ball you've earned

The point is won when your opponent is out of position or forced to hit up. Your job is to engineer that situation through placement, not to end the point directly.

## By court zone

| Zone | Goal | How |
|------|------|-----|
| Kitchen | Move opponent side to side | [Side-to-side dinking](side-to-side-dinking.md) until a high ball appears |
| Transition | Keep ball low at feet | Aim center of box, hit down |
| Baseline | Drop, slow down, create angles | [Court angles](court-angles.md) after establishing center |

## The one exception

Hit hard **only** when contact is above the net — see [attack trigger](attack-trigger.md). Every other ball: place it.
