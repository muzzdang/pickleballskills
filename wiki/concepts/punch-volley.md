# Punch Volley

## What It Is

A compact, attacking volley at the kitchen line — specifically the backhand version. The goal is to apply pressure without over-swinging. It replaces the reset on balls that are high enough to attack.

The shot is named for its feel: a forward punch with a locked wrist, not a swing, slice, or flick.

## The Core Motion: "Door Closed, Door Open"

- **Door closed:** Start with the paddle parallel to the net — face square, paddle in front of the body.
- **Door open:** Push the paddle forward toward the target. The face "opens" slightly as it moves forward.
- **Return to ready:** After contact, return the paddle to the closed parallel position.

The "open/close" refers to the minimal swing arc — not a big rotation. The paddle moves forward, not sideways.

## Technique

### 1. Wrist — lock it
The most common error. The wrist must be **completely locked** before contact and stay locked through the swing. A loose wrist lets the face angle change at contact, sending the ball up or sideways.

Drill: push the top of the paddle tip forward and stop it in front of you — this trains the locked-wrist, forward-push feel.

### 2. Elbow — stay close to body
When the elbow drifts high or outward, the wrist automatically breaks. Elbow tucked = wrist locked = consistent face.

### 3. Takeback — nothing past the shoulder
No big backswing. Start flat and push forward. At the kitchen you don't need to load up — compact is fast and consistent.

### 4. Stay square to the net
Don't turn the body excessively sideways to generate power. Power comes from the compact forward push, not body rotation. Too much turn → ball goes sideways, not forward.

### 5. Lean into the shot
Weight forward, not on heels. Forward lean produces better contact and better direction control.

### Face Angle by Ball Height

| Ball height | Face angle | Motion |
|---|---|---|
| Chest and above | Flat/neutral | Push straight forward |
| Mid-chest / stomach | Slight upward tilt | Push forward and down toward feet |
| Hip level | More tilt, slight turn | "Gabe Tardio style" — more angled, slightly more rotation |

## When to Punch vs. Reset

- **Punch:** Ball is at an attackable height (above knee, not dipping below comfortable contact)
- **Reset or flick:** Ball is dipping low — "if it's dipping, I'm going to think about resetting or hitting a flick"

Don't force the punch on balls below comfortable contact. The punch is a pressure shot, not a survival shot.

## Common Errors

| Error | Effect | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Loose wrist | Ball pops up or goes wide | Lock wrist before and through contact |
| Elbow drifting high/out | Forces wrist to break | Keep elbow close to body |
| Too much body turn | Ball goes sideways | Stay square; push, don't swing |
| Big backswing | Late contact, loss of control | Nothing past shoulder; start flat |
| Back on heels | Weak contact | Lean into the shot |
