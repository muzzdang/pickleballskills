# Ready Position

The foundational stance between shots. The ready position is not a static pose — it's a dynamic reset that must happen after every single shot. Players who fail to return to ready position after each contact are perpetually "behind" the play.

## The Three Benchmark Levels (Austin Hardy / PPB)

Austin Hardy identifies three progressively advanced ready positions based on observed pro mechanics:

### Level 1 — JW (Beginner)
- Paddle held flat and chest-high
- Arms close to the body
- Weight centered, knees slightly bent
- Suitable for beginners learning to keep the paddle up

### Level 2 — Ben Johns (Intermediate)
- Paddle angled slightly open, held at waist height
- Elbow bent and in front of the body
- Weight slightly forward on the balls of the feet
- More responsive than the JW position because the paddle is already in the contact zone

### Level 3 — Anna Leigh Waters (Advanced)
- Paddle slightly open, held low (below waist, near hip height)
- Arms relaxed and away from the body — a natural hang position
- Weight forward, athletic "coil" in the legs
- Appears passive but is actually pre-loaded; any ball above this position is already in the strike zone

## Hayden Patriquin's Variation (per PPB)

Austin's breakdown of Hayden's footage shows elbows out and away from the body (not tucked), paddle slightly open. After every single shot — whether a dink, a reset, or a drive — Hayden's hands return to this exact position before the opponent contacts the ball. This makes his ready position a practiced ritual, not a reaction.

## Key Principles

- **Reset after every shot.** The most common error is allowing the paddle to drift low or to the side after contact and never recovering before the next ball arrives.
- **Elbows out, not tucked.** Tucked elbows shorten reach and slow reaction time. Elbows out creates a wider "paddle coverage zone."
- **Paddle face slightly open.** An open face pre-positions the paddle for the most common dink/reset contact angle. A closed face means re-adjusting under pressure.
- **Weight forward.** Heels on the ground = no reactive ability. Balls of the feet = immediate directional movement.
