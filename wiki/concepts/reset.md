# Reset

A soft, controlled shot designed to neutralize an opponent's attack and return the rally to a neutral dink exchange. The reset is not a passive shot — it is the correct aggressive choice when attacking is not available.

## When to Reset

The reset is the correct shot when:
- You are off-balance or reaching
- The ball is below the net tape (attacking it sends it into the net or sails long)
- The opponent has a strong attacking position and any hard ball will be countered
- You are in the transition zone and the ball is not clearly attackable

**Austin Hardy's rule:** If Monitor (MFWC step 1) tells you the ball is not attackable — reset. Do not try to attack from a reaching position. ([5 Rules Ben Johns & Anna Leigh Use](../sources/2026-04-06-gBGRF6IzKQg-5-rules-ben-johns-anna-leigh.md): "Cut when reaching, topspin when in position.")

## Mechanics

- **Absorb, don't punch:** The reset is a soft deceleration, not a swing. The paddle face absorbs the pace of the incoming ball and redirects it with minimal pace.
- **Open paddle face:** Face slightly open to loft the ball over the net with a gentle arc
- **Contact in front of body:** Never behind the body or at the side
- **Feet set if possible:** A reset from a reaching position is lower-percentage; get the feet behind the ball whenever time allows

## Reset vs. Dink

The distinction:
- A **dink** is hit from a neutral position in a dink rally — proactive placement
- A **reset** is hit in response to an attack or difficult ball — reactive neutralization

In practice, a successful reset looks like a dink — it lands soft in the kitchen. The difference is the situation that prompted it.

## Transition Zone Resets

When caught in no man's land and attacked:
- Do not try to drive or attack the ball back — this is almost always an error
- Execute a reset (soft, low ball into the kitchen) and continue advancing
- The reset earns you the right to keep moving forward; a failed attack sends you back to baseline
- See [MFWC System](mfwc-system.md) for the decision framework
