# Roll Shot

An aggressive topspin shot hit at or near the kitchen line. The goal is to drive the ball down at the opponent's feet with heavy topspin, forcing a defensive pop-up.

## Technique

1. **Get extra low** — drop your body well below the ball; you need room to brush up
2. **Shoulder initiates** — start the motion with the shoulder
3. **[Wrist lag](wrist-lag.md) then snap** — wrist lags behind, then releases through contact
4. **Brush up steeply** — generates [topspin](topspin.md), which is essential because there's almost no court left to land in
5. **Loose [grip pressure](grip-pressure.md)** until the snap

## Why topspin is non-negotiable here

At the kitchen, you're hitting from a low position into a very short court. Without topspin, the ball sails long. The steep topspin trajectory is what makes the shot land safely.

## Applies to both wings

The backhand roll follows the same pattern — shoulder leads, wrist lags and snaps.

## Roll Volley (4th Shot Application)

The roll shot also appears as a **4th shot option** when the serving team's 3rd shot arrives mid-level or higher and you've reached the NVZ. In this context:
- Contact is out in front of the body, roughly at waist height or slightly below the net
- Weight is forward, leaning into the kitchen
- The topspin arc brings the ball down at the opponent's feet as they're still transitioning
- The goal is keeping them back, not resetting — this is an offensive continuation

See: [Fourth Shot](fourth-shot.md)

## Roll vs. Backhand Flick

The roll and the [backhand flick](backhand-flick.md) are distinct shots often confused:

| | Roll | Backhand Flick |
|---|---|---|
| Power source | Shoulder-driven | Wrist-driven |
| Paddle motion | Smooth follow-through | Windshield wiper, stop with velocity |
| Feel | Continuous, rhythmic | Snappy, compact |

[Cori Elliott](../entities/cori-elliott.md) corrects students who blend both: the roll paddle face stays relatively flat through contact; the flick paddle face closes over the ball. Mixing the two creates unpredictable direction.
