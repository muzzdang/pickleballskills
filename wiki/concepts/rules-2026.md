# 2026 Rule Changes

Key pickleball rule updates effective 2026 that players need to know.

## Line calls

**No spectator consultation** — changed from "should not" to **must not** consult spectators. All calls by players on the court only.

**Conflict = ball in** — if both partners disagree on a call (one says in, one says out), the ball defaults to **in**.

**No delayed calls** — out calls must be made before the opponent hits the ball again. Play it, then wait to call it out → that's now a fault.

**Opponent override** — if you ask your opponent whether a ball was in or out, whatever they say **stands**, overriding your own opinion.

## Serve rules (clarified with "clearly")

Three requirements are now stricter — the gray areas are gone:
1. **Upward arc** — paddle must clearly be moving upward at contact (no ambiguous half-swings)
2. **Paddle head below wrist** — the paddle head must clearly be below the wrist joint at contact
3. **Contact below waist** — contact must clearly be below the waist

**Spin on serve:** You may not spin the ball before serving. You may apply spin with the paddle at the moment of contact. This was always the rule but is now clarified.

## Multi-contact (new)

The ball can contact your paddle **multiple times** in a single continuous stroke — this is now explicitly legal as long as it's continuous and not a carry (rolling across the face). Previously only a double-hit in continuous motion was legal.

## Net post rule reversal

When a ball hits the post: the point goes to whichever team's **court** the ball bounced on before hitting the post (not whichever team hit it). This theoretically enables a backspin shot that bounces on the opponent's side and hits the post — they lose the point.

## Injury timeout / 1v2

If a doubles partner is injured and can't continue after 15 minutes, the remaining player may continue the match alone against both opponents. Normal rules apply except: points automatically lost when the missing partner would have served or returned.

## Visible ball penalty

A ball in your pocket that becomes visible mid-rally = immediate point loss. A ball or object falling from a pocket onto the court = immediate point loss. Don't carry extra balls in pockets.
