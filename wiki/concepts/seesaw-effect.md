# Seesaw Effect

Adjust paddle height based on where you are on the court. As you move from baseline to kitchen, paddle position rises from low (hips) to high (chest).

## By zone

| Zone | Paddle height | Why |
|------|--------------|-----|
| Baseline | Low, near hips | Opponents aim at your feet; ready for groundstrokes |
| Transition | Mid, near belly button | Must handle both low rolls at feet and occasional pop-up volleys |
| Kitchen | High, chest height | Fast exchanges; prioritize volley readiness; dinks are slow enough to drop and reset |

## As an out-ball indicator

The paddle position doubles as an out-call guide:
- **Baseline:** if the ball is above your hip-high paddle → likely out
- **Transition:** if a ball is coming at chest height with room behind you → probably out; let it go
- **Kitchen:** ball above your chest-high paddle with room behind → give it space

## Priority at the kitchen

At the kitchen, keep paddle chest-high even though dinking requires a low contact. Reason: speedups are faster and more dangerous than dinks. You have time to drop low for dinks (they're slow); you don't have time to raise up from a low position for a speedup counter.
