# Side-to-Side Dinking

A kitchen strategy where you systematically move your opponent left and right with dinks until they're off-balance and pop a ball up. The pop-up is the earned opportunity to attack — don't force it before then.

## The pattern

Rather than dinking straight back to the same spot, alternate between your opponent's left foot and right foot. This forces lateral movement, degrades their balance, and eventually produces a high ball you can attack.

The mantra: **create the opportunity, don't force it.**

## Drills

**Left foot / right foot drill** — Both players dink crosscourt. Each player aims alternately at their opponent's left foot then right foot. Builds the habit of intentional shot placement over mindless rallying.

**Down-the-line / crosscourt drill** — One player hits every dink down the line; the other goes crosscourt. Then switch. Trains the brain to recognize court patterns and develop intentional shot selection.

## Why random speedups fail

At higher levels, opponents anticipate speedups and counter them. Even at lower levels, a well-timed speedup into a ready opponent results in a counter you can't handle. The higher-percentage play is to earn a weak ball through movement.
