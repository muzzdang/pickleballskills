# Singles Pickleball

A one-on-one format of pickleball played on the same court as doubles, but with different scoring and positional strategy. As of 2026, singles at the PPA Challenger level is being played on a **17-foot wide court** (narrowed from the standard 20-foot width).

## 17-Foot Court (2026 Innovation)

### Background
USA Pickleball and the PPA began testing a shortened singles court in early 2026, implemented at the Challenger (developmental/amateur) level. The 17-foot width matches a standard **badminton singles court** exactly — providing a ready-made template that venues already have lined on their floors.

### Why 17 Feet?

The standard 20-foot court makes singles play heavily power-dependent: the open court is wide enough that most drops and resets can be neutralized by stepping in and attacking. The narrowed court:
- Makes drops and resets viable again — less open court to attack into
- Creates longer dink rallies and more "cat and mouse" play at the kitchen
- Rewards consistency and placement over raw power
- Creates more strategic depth overall

Austin Hardy's live review of 17-foot singles play showed longer rallies, more kitchen exchanges, and fewer outright winners compared to standard singles.

### Adoption Status (as of 2026)
- PPA Challenger level: **implemented**
- PPA Tour (professional) level: **not yet adopted**
- Austin expects broader adoption if early data shows improved viewership and game quality

## Scoring in Singles
- Standard games to 11, win by 2
- Rally scoring is being piloted at some events (to 21)
- Server announces score before each serve

## Strategy Differences from Doubles

Singles is far more physically demanding. Key differences:
- No partner to cover the middle — every shot must be covered solo
- The drop becomes more important because a drive gives the opponent the entire open court
- Lob is a critical reset weapon — the opponent cannot cover the entire court on a deep lob
- Fitness and footwork are far more decisive factors than in doubles
