# Slice

A slice shot uses a high-to-low swing path with an open paddle face to put backspin (underspin) on the ball. The slice is a versatile tool used in returns, third shot drops, dinks, and as a defensive reset option.

## How It Works

- **Swing path:** high-to-low — the paddle cuts underneath the ball
- **Paddle face:** open (angled back/upward) so the face makes glancing contact with the underside of the ball
- **Result:** backspin causes the ball to spin backward as it travels forward — it decelerates after bouncing and stays low

## Applications

### Slice Return
A popular return against fast or awkward serves. The slice keeps the ball low, which makes the server's third shot more difficult. See [Slice Return](slice-return.md).

### Slice Third Shot Drop
The slice drop floats the ball low over the net with backspin. The ball skids low after bouncing and tends to make the opponent pop up when they attack it. More deceptive than a topspin drop but harder to master.

See: [Topspin & Slice 3rd Shot Drops (Universal Rackets, 2026-04-10)](../sources/2026-04-10-universal-rackets-topspin-slice-3rd-shot-drops.md)

### Slice Dink
A low-spinning dink that stays low over the net. Covered in [Bump Dink](bump-dink.md) — the bump dink is presented as an alternative to the slice dink for controlling the kitchen rally.

### Wind Conditions
Slice is more reliable than topspin in wind — the lower trajectory stays under wind currents rather than ballooning. See [Windy Conditions](windy-conditions.md).

## Spin Continuation Interaction

When you return a topspin shot with a slice, the ball arrives at the opponent with backspin — which, combined with the opponent's incoming topspin, creates a complex bounce. This is the [spin continuation](spin-continuation.md) principle: use the opposite spin type to create a difficult incoming ball.

## Common Errors

- **Too much face angle:** extreme open face sends the ball too high — it floats and becomes attackable
- **Scooping instead of cutting:** swinging upward under the ball (scooping) creates a different spin than a clean cut; the result is unpredictable
- **Using slice when topspin is safer:** in a fast exchange, the topspin drop has more margin; slice drop is a deliberate choice requiring setup time
