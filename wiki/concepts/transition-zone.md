# Transition Zone

The area between the baseline and the kitchen line where a player is moving forward after their third or fifth shot. This is the most vulnerable position on the court — the player is moving, off-balance, and covering a large rectangle of space.

## How to exploit it

When your opponent is in transition, **target their feet**. Keep the ball low by hitting down into the court. A ball at the feet forces a difficult low contact and typically produces a pop-up or a weak reply.

**Aim center of the box** — not at the lines, not at the baseline. A large center target gives you the highest margin for error while still landing at their feet regardless of which way the ball drifts.

**In doubles:** always hit to the person furthest back. The net player can put away anything above the tape — never feed them.

## Hit behind moving opponents

Good players will follow the ball as they move in. Aim to the side they're moving *away* from — there's always an open lane. See [hit behind](hit-behind.md).

## Drill

**Center out wide:** Hit alternating balls — one to the center, one out wide — while the drilling partner resets every ball. Builds the habit of varying placement rather than defaulting to one spot.

## When to Advance (as the returning team)

Two separate triggers — one for drives, one for resets:

**After a drive:** Don't go automatically. Watch the ball leave your paddle. If you can see it *dipping* as it crosses the net, trust it and move in. If you can't read the flight yet, wait.

**After a reset:** Don't advance just because you hit a reset. Only move forward when your ball is *meaningfully lower* than the previous exchange. Hitting from the same spot at the same height is correct — you're waiting for the opening, not creating it by moving. Moving forward during a neutral reset causes your next shot to be hit while moving and typically sails out.

**The drop is the most reliable advance trigger** — a quality drop forces the opponent to hit up, giving you a ball you can step into confidently. See [Third Shot Drop](third-shot-drop.md).
