# Volley

A volley is any shot struck before the ball bounces. In pickleball, volleys can only be hit outside the Non-Volley Zone (NVZ/kitchen). The volley is primarily a placement shot — the pace is already provided by the opponent.

## Forehand Volley

**Mechanics:**
- Compact punch forward — no backswing at the kitchen
- Elbow in front of the body, not swinging from behind
- Contact point ahead of the body in line with the front knee
- Wrist firms at contact for direction control

**Putaway volley:** When the ball is above net height and clearly attackable, the forehand volley becomes a finishing shot. Wrist snap generates pace, paddle aims at a corner or the body gap.

See: [Forehand Putaway Volley (Universal Rackets, 2026-01-01)](../sources/2026-01-01-universal-rackets-forehand-putaway-volley.md)

## Backhand Volley

Generally the weaker volley for recreational players. Key mechanics:
- Elbow tucks toward body — don't let it fly out
- Face angle is critical: square face for a block, slightly open face for a soft redirect
- Less wrist action than the forehand volley — the redirection comes from [paddle face direction](paddle-face-direction.md)

See the [Punch Volley](punch-volley.md) page for the backhand punch volley technique from Cori Elliott.

## Fast-Hands Volley Exchanges

In fast kitchen exchanges, there is no time for a swing. The volley becomes a redirect:
- Paddle stays up and slightly in front
- Use the incoming ball's pace; absorb and redirect
- Direction comes from where the face points, not from the swing

## When Attacked: Defensive Volleys

When the opponent speeds up a ball at you:
- **Option 1:** Absorb and redirect to their feet — use [reset mechanics](reset-mechanics.md)
- **Option 2:** Block the ball back low — deny them the put-away, neutralize the exchange
- **Option 3:** Counter-attack if you can get the paddle face on the ball with control
