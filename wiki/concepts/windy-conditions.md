# Windy Conditions

Pickleball's hollow plastic ball is highly sensitive to wind — more so than tennis balls or shuttlecocks because of the holes and light weight. Wind fundamentally changes ball flight and requires adjusted shot selection and positioning.

## Core Principle

Turn the wind into an advantage rather than fighting it as a problem. Wind affects both teams equally — the player who adapts faster wins.

## Shot Selection Adjustments

**Serving into the wind:**
- The ball will slow and drop more quickly — serves may land shorter than expected
- Add more pace or topspin to counteract the ball dying in the wind
- Stand slightly further back when receiving; returns will come shorter

**Serving with the wind (tailwind):**
- Ball carries further — risk of serving long
- Reduce pace slightly; rely on accuracy over power
- Opponents' returns will also carry — be ready for deeper returns

**Topspin vs. Slice in wind:**
- **Slice/backspin** is more reliable in wind — lower trajectory stays under the wind
- **Topspin** (especially on drives) can balloon badly when hit into a headwind — the topspin spin interacts with the headwind to create lift
- In windy conditions, favor [slice](slice.md) or flatter shots over heavy topspin

**Dinking in wind:**
- Dinking into the wind: requires less pace than normal — the wind decelerates the ball; a light push is enough
- Dinking with the wind: requires more controlled touch — ball carries further than expected
- Adjust early: take a few practice swings mentally noting how much the ball is moving

## Positioning Adjustments

- When serving into the wind, stand slightly further back from the baseline — returns will come shorter and you need the range
- At the kitchen in crosswind, track the ball more carefully — lateral drift can move the ball 6-12 inches from expected trajectory

## Mental Game

The biggest adjustment is psychological: accept that normal shots will behave differently, and slow down your decision-making to account for ball movement. Players who get frustrated at "bad luck" in wind have already lost the mental battle.
