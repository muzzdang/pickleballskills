# Wrist Lag

A technique where the wrist stays behind the paddle path at the start of the swing, then snaps forward through contact to deliver maximum speed at impact. Used in serves and [roll shots](roll-shot.md) at the kitchen.

## How it works

The shoulder initiates the motion, but the wrist deliberately lags behind. As contact approaches, the wrist releases and snaps forward — like cracking a whip. The lag builds elastic energy; the snap releases it.

## Where it applies

- **Serves** — shoulder leads, wrist lags and snaps for pace
- **Kitchen roll shots** — same motion; combined with getting low under the ball to generate [topspin](topspin.md)
- **Backhand rolls** — same principle applies

## Relationship to grip pressure

Wrist lag only works if [grip pressure](grip-pressure.md) is loose during the lag phase. A tight grip kills the snap.
