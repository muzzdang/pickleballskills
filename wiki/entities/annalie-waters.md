# Annalie Waters

**Type:** Professional pickleball player
**Nationality:** American
**Known for:** Dominant two-handed backhand; kitchen-line control; featured frequently in women's and mixed doubles highlights

## Playing Style

Annalie Waters is consistently featured in elite firefight compilations. Commentators highlight her **two-handed backhand** as a defensive wall — "there's no place to really attack" when she holds the middle with it. She is noted for the ability to stay in the longest firefights and eventually win them through endurance and shot quality rather than pure pace.

A firefight between Annalie and Lacy Schneemann was called "point of the match" — notable for both the recoils and the ability to sustain pressure at a 4/10-second reaction window.

## Signature Shot: Lob Serve (2026)

Anna Leigh pioneered the **lob serve** at the pro level in early 2026, debuting it in tournament play and causing significant disruption. Key features:
- Open paddle face at contact; brushing motion back toward the body (not driving through)
- Must land within last 2–3 feet of baseline to be effective
- Used predominantly odd-side to odd-side — targets opponent's backhand and pulls them off court
- Lethal against [stacking](../concepts/stacking.md) teams — compounds the positional swap delay
- Counter: take it off the bounce at the rise in the strike zone

See: [Lob Serve](../concepts/lob-serve.md)

## Ready Position (PPB Level 3)

Austin Hardy uses Anna Leigh's ready position as the "Level 3" (advanced) benchmark: paddle slightly open, held low near hip height, arms relaxed away from the body. Appears passive but is pre-loaded — any ball above this position is already in the strike zone.

## Vulnerability (per Hayden Patriquin analysis)

Austin Hardy notes that Anna Leigh's two-handed backhand has a weakness at balls directed at her right hip — the ball forces an awkward swing with the elbow in an inefficient position. Hayden Patriquin deliberately exploits this pattern.

## Partnership with Ben Johns

As part of the top mixed doubles pair, Anna Leigh and Ben use the [string theory](../concepts/middle-strategy.md) movement system — mirroring each other's lateral shifts to eliminate the middle gap. They were notably defeated by Hayden Patriquin and Anna Bright in a match where the opponents hit exclusively to the middle.
