# Beyond Measure Ronin

**Brand:** Beyond Measure
**Model:** Ronin
**Shapes:** Hybrid, Elongated
**Core/Generation:** Gen 4
**Face:** T700 carbon fiber
**Handle:** Short (notably shorter than most paddles)
**Price:** ~retail minus 10% with code PLAYBOOK
**Category:** Control / All-court

## Visual Profile

- Dark charcoal/graphite face
- Teal/cyan accent color
- "RONIN" in white on face; "BEYOND MEASURE" printed vertically on edge tape
- Hybrid: rounder, wider head shape
- Elongated: taller, narrower profile
- Both models share identical feel profile

## Performance Profile

| Category | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Power | ★★★☆☆ | Balanced; enough to finish points; pop higher than raw power |
| Spin | ★★★★☆ | T700 carbon fiber; typical T700 output (same as Luzz Inferno) |
| Control | ★★★★★ | Very high; huge sweet spot; plush/marshmallow feel |
| Sweet spot | ★★★★★ | Massive; only the very tip is a dead zone |
| Feel | ★★★★★ | Soft dwell, ball "sinks into face then comes out"; highly tactile |

## Key Characteristics

- **Marshmallow feel** — significant dwell time, very soft contact. Reviewers describe feeling each shot distinctly through the paddle.
- **Massive sweet spot** — off-center hits feel nearly identical to center hits; only the extreme tip is truly dead.
- **Short handle** — the main weakness noted; two-handed backhand users will feel cramped. Consider trying in-hand before committing.
- **Extend into shots** — unlike power paddles, control paddles like the Ronin require you to extend and lean forward on drops/dinks rather than just blocking; the softness means you need to add more motion than you're used to.
- **Pop when needed** — despite the soft feel, flicking at the NVZ sends the ball away cleanly; "I can really just stick it here and it really travels."

## Comparisons

- **Friday Aura** — similar feel; Ronin is a step above with more control
- **Aura Pro** — Ronin sits between Aura and Aura Pro
- **Loco (bread and butter)** — similar balanced, control-oriented feel
- **Luzz Cannon** — opposite end of the spectrum; see [Luzz Cannon](luzz-cannon.md)

## Who It's For

- Control players, kitchen-first styles
- Advanced players who prioritize feel over power
- Intermediates who want a forgiving paddle to develop touch
