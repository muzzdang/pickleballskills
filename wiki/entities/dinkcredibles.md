# Dinkcredibles (Kieran)

**Type:** YouTube channel / coaching brand
**Host:** Kieran (last name not provided in sources)
**Background:** D1 college tennis player; certified strength and conditioning specialist (CSCS); pickleball coach
**Channel:** Dinkcredibles (YouTube)
**Partnership:** Recurring guest coach on [Pickleball Playbook](pickleballplaybook.md) with Austin Hardy

## Teaching Philosophy

Kieran's approach merges athletic movement science with pickleball mechanics. His core premise: most pickleball errors are not paddle problems — they are **body mechanics problems**. Fixing the foundation (stance, foot loading, hip rotation) resolves the paddle issue without any paddle-specific coaching.

His drilling methodology follows a strict **cooperative → cooperative-competitive → competitive** progression. Skipping levels causes players to default to bad habits under pressure.

## Key Concepts and Drills

### Foundational Cues
- **Legs = springs:** Legs absorb and generate energy (like springs). Never rigid.
- **Arm = ribbon:** The arm stays loose through dink contact. Tension kills touch.
- **Semi-open stance:** 45 degrees to the net for dinks; loads hips for rotation.
- **Tripod foot:** Three-point weight distribution — heel, ball under big toe, ball under pinky toe. Activates glutes, stabilizes ankle-knee-hip chain.

### Dink Drills
- Dink tosses (arm path without paddle)
- Paddle half-hits (top half of face only)
- Scoop and catch (feel the finish position)
- Skinny singles cooperative (100 consecutive dinks)

### Body Exercises for Backhand Dink Fix
Five exercises targeting root causes of the backhand dink yip:
1. Adductor release (inner thigh flexibility)
2. Outside leg stabilization (single-leg balance)
3. Tripod foot loading
4. Hip swivel through contact
5. Knee tracking over toe

### Tournament Preparation Protocol
- Night before: low intensity only — light ball touching, no hard drilling
- Day of: graduated warm-up sequence (dynamic movement → soft dinks → transitions → full pace)
- Daily off-court: 5 minutes shadow footwork in front of mirror

### Bosnian Volleys
A volley drill with exaggerated open paddle face at half speed — trains correct contact angle. Named after its Eastern European tennis training origin.
