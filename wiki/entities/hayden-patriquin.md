# Hayden Patriquin

**Type:** Professional pickleball player
**Nationality:** American
**Known for:** Elite hands-battle mechanics; strategic middle targeting; body-bag attacks; defeating Ben Johns / Anna Leigh Waters in high-profile matches

## Playing Style

Hayden Patriquin is one of the most strategically sophisticated players in professional pickleball. Austin Hardy ([Pickleball Playbook](pickleballplaybook.md)) has analyzed his footage extensively, identifying him as the elite benchmark for hands-battle mechanics and tactical decision-making.

## Hands-Battle Mechanics (per PPB Analysis)

Three key mechanical elements distinguish Hayden's kitchen game:

- **Elbows out and away from the body** — never tucked. This creates a wider paddle coverage zone and faster effective reach without lunging.
- **Sandwich position (forehand volleys)** — paddle face closes slightly with the elbow driving through contact, as if sandwiching the ball between the paddle and an imaginary wall.
- **Eagle position (backhand volleys)** — arm extends outward like a wing, elbow leads, contact out in front. Named for the winglike arm shape.

After every single shot — dink, reset, or drive — Hayden returns his hands to the same ready position before the opponent contacts the ball. This ritualistic reset is what makes his preparation appear effortless.

## Strategic Tendencies

### Middle Targeting Against Ben Johns
Hayden's primary tactic against [Ben Johns](ben-johns.md) is to target every ball up the middle seam. This exploits communication uncertainty between Ben's partnership and removes Ben's ability to step around for an inside-out forehand. Hayden and Anna Bright defeated Ben and [Anna Leigh Waters](annalie-waters.md) in a notable match using this approach exclusively.

### Cover Middle with Forehand
Hayden takes the middle with his forehand aggressively — even balls that appear to be his partner's. His forehand is strong enough that the poach is a net positive even accounting for partner disruption.

### Body-Bag Attack
Hayden deliberately targets opponents' bodies (hip, shoulder, torso) during hands battles rather than chasing open court. Balls at the body are the hardest to deflect cleanly. This is a documented pattern, not random shot selection.

### Anna Leigh Waters Exploit
Austin Hardy notes that Hayden specifically targets Anna Leigh's right hip — a documented vulnerability in her two-handed backhand that forces an awkward swing with the elbow in an inefficient position.
