# Luzz Cannon

**Brand:** Luzz
**Model:** Cannon (Elongated)
**Price:** ~$99 retail / ~$92 with discount codes
**Core:** Gen 3
**Shape:** Elongated
**Category:** Max power

## Specs (from on-screen card)

- Shape: Elongated
- Core: Gen 3 thermoformed
- Price: $99 (one of the cheapest Gen 3 power paddles)

## Performance Profile

| Category | Rating | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Power | ★★★★★ | Possibly the hardest-hitting Gen 3 paddle available |
| Spin | ★★★★☆ | Above average |
| Control | ★★☆☆☆ | Sacrificed heavily for power |
| Sweet spot | ★★☆☆☆ | Smaller than expected; mishits go completely dead |
| Feel | ★★☆☆☆ | Off-frame hits are dead; needs lead tape |

## Key Characteristics

- **Effortless power from the baseline** — shots launch with minimal effort
- **Loud "donk" sound** — one of the most audibly distinctive paddles
- **Significant break-in period** — unusable for many players out of the box; becomes more manageable after 15-30 minutes
- **Compact motions mandatory** — any extra wrist or swing sends the ball long; drops/resets require near-perfect mechanics
- **Mishit penalty:** Off-center hits go completely dead (no trampoline). Lead tape recommended to widen sweet spot.

## Who It's For

- Baseline bangers around the 4.0 level
- Older players who need help generating power
- Recreational players (1-2x/week) who prioritize fun over precision

## Who Should Avoid It

- Kitchen-first players; dink-heavy styles
- Players still developing control
- Anyone who relies on drops, touch, or resets as their primary shots
