# PaddleTek Honeyfoam (TKO-X 16mm)

**Type:** Pickleball paddle
**Brand:** PaddleTek
**Price:** $250 (list); limited by fall 2026 due to Yola lawsuit settlement
**Certification:** 16mm = USAPA + UPAA dual; 14mm = UPAA only

## Specs

- **Core**: EPP foam center + narrow horseshoe EVA perimeter ring (narrower than most foam paddles)
- **Face**: carbon fiber
- **Shape**: elongated only (as of 2026-05-20)
- **Thickness**: 16mm (primary) and 14mm variants
- **Swing weight**: ~118
- **Handle length**: 5.75 in (generous — good for two-handed backhand without finger on back)
- **Grip size**: 4.25 in (chunky/blocky — not ideal for smaller hands)

## Notes

Identical to the **Paddletek Reserve** (the limited-edition early-access version); Reserve owners do not need to repurchase. Yola named this paddle in their Gen 3 design lawsuit; Paddletek settled to pay royalties and must discontinue sales in fall 2026 — stock up if you love it.

## Feel Profile

Soft and dense — more muted and dampened than the stiff-hollow power paddles dominating the 2026 market. Dynamic power profile: putting something into the ball yields more; small swings yield less. This makes it feel "controlled" at low swing speeds and "powerful" at full speed — a relatively uncommon combination. Low-tier power categorization.

Compared to [Friday Aura](../entities/friday-aura.md): Honeyfoam has more pop and power ceiling; Aura is half the price in the same soft-dense category. Sweet spot slightly below average for an elongated; improvable with corner weight.

## Community Verdict

Good paddle in an underserved market niche, but overpriced without a durable grit surface. Players coming from the old Bantam will find it a significant upgrade. The soft-dense feel is increasingly rare as the market trends stiff-hollow.
