# Proton Peacock

**Type:** Pickleball paddle
**Brand:** Proton
**Original price:** $280; **New price:** $127+ (after restructuring + code "kitchen" for 15% off)
**Certification:** Not specified in source; targeted at broad amateur market

## Specs

- **Core**: Full foam Gen 4 — "atomic foam core" center + EVA foam perimeter
- **Face**: carbon fiber
- **Shapes**: elongated and widebody (square)
- **Thicknesses**: 13mm and 15mm (both less common than the standard 16mm/14mm)
- **Reviewed unit (elongated 15mm)**: 8.09 oz static weight, swing weight 118.9, twist weight 6.4
- **Handle (elongated)**: 5.25 in — shorter than many elongateds
- **Design**: bold, bright, eye-catching — Proton brand identity

## Feel Profile

Consistent and well-rounded; "firm but not sharp"; slightly muted feel despite thinner-than-average cores. Sits in the lower-mid tier of power paddles — doesn't stand out in any single category but doesn't fail in any. Notably: thinner cores typically yield stiffer, poppier feel, but the Peacock's construction keeps it controlled.

**15mm vs 13mm**: 15mm has better swing weight, more forgiveness, more dwell — recommended for most players. 13mm widebody has very low swing weight and high twist weight — specialized weapon for advanced net play and hand speed; sacrifices some power and soft touch.

## Brand Story

Proton's background is in softball, bringing a different R&D perspective. Community-focused: events, pro access, charity through Good Sports Foundation (sports equipment for kids worldwide), flagship stores in Vietnam as community hubs, title sponsor of the junior PPA tour.

**Lifetime warranty** — rare in the industry; significantly improves long-term value proposition especially now at the new price point.

## Community Verdict

A good paddle that was overpriced at $280; at $127 it becomes a serious consideration. Consistent and approachable; the lifetime warranty and unique shape/thickness options differentiate it from commodity Gen 4 paddles.
