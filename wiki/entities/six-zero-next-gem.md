# Six Zero — Next Gem Lineup

Six Zero's current paddle family: Coral, Black Opal, and Ruby Pro. All feature Diamond Tough Grit (extended surface life) but differ in core, power level, and target player.

## Diamond Tough Grit

Diamond-infused surface claimed to last 4× longer than standard carbon fiber. Spin generation starts strong and degrades more slowly. Present in all three Next Gem paddles.

## Paddle comparison

| Paddle | Core | Thickness | Power | Best for |
|--------|------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Black Opal | EPP foam | 14mm hybrid | Highest (comparable to Selkirk Boomstick) | Advanced players who want max power; requires adjustment |
| Ruby Pro | Polypropylene | 14mm hybrid | Mid; spin/touch focused | Control-oriented players; fans of original Ruby |
| Coral | EPP foam | 16mm (all shapes) | Mid-high; all-court | Most players; natural step up from Double Black Diamond |

## Coral highlights

- Most accessible and user-friendly in the lineup
- Tectonic Core Suspension System: EP foam with EVA ring, incisions in grid pattern for responsive dwell
- All three shapes available (only Coral comes in standard, hybrid, elongated)
- Gets better with a small amount of added weight at lower corners
- $200 (10% off with code KITCHEN)
- Bobby's pick for most players who don't specifically need max power or all-polypropylene feel

## Black Opal specifics

- 14mm hybrid only
- Power level at or slightly above Boomstick in serve speed tests
- Not recommended below ~4.0 — the pop can cause control issues
- Power Gel layer (not in Coral) adds extra pop

## Ruby Pro specifics

- Polypropylene core (only non-foam in lineup)
- Reimagined version of the original Ruby
- Best if you dislike foam feel or prefer control/touch over power
