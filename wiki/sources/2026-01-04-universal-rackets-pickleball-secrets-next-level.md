## Key Takeaways

Tyler shares the specific breakthroughs from his 2025 season — all from process changes, not more drilling:

- **Move back to create space for speed-ups, not just to get sideways.** The insight: stepping back makes contact happen out in front. Previously Tyler moved back but still got the ball close; now he steps back and hits with full extension. Non-dominant hand drill works here too — wherever you can get the non-dominant hand, that's where contact should be.
- **Two-handed block for incoming drives.** When former top-100 tennis players drove at Tyler, he couldn't handle it one-handed. Solution: two hands on the paddle, weight set, block back. This stabilizes the paddle face and eliminates flinching. Controversial tip that got pushback, but it worked.
- **Backhand dink: turn the upper body, keep the paddle front.** Tyler's revelation: instead of turning body AND taking paddle back (which causes jamming), keep the paddle in ready position and rotate the upper body — that rotation automatically repositions the paddle. Result: "unstoppable" backhand dinks.
- **Driving at 60–70% power.** Counterintuitive for a tennis player: slower drives are more consistent and create the same short-reply goal. Drill: stand at baseline with partner, hit drives back and forth at 60% — the drill Tyler credits for fixing his drive. Not practiced enough by players who only drill at the kitchen.
- **Hybrid drive-drop (the "drip").** Watching Quang Dong (Q): roll the ball rather than just driving or dropping. Look like a drive, come up on the ball last moment. Added his finger on top of the paddle (like Anna-Leigh and Q) to get more brush and control — "where have you been all my life."
- **Disguised speed-up off the dink.** Pattern: dink → dink → step back → speed up through the middle. Then once opponents read it, go straight ahead instead to keep them honest. This is the late-2025 tactical breakthrough.
- **Serving at 50–60% to start, then escalate.** Stop trying to kill every serve from point 1. Build in, then when feeling it: serve big and immediately take a step in, knowing the short reply is coming.
- **Poaching the middle: if you're closer to the kitchen, take the ball.** Previously Tyler deferred to avoid being a ball hog. New rule: whoever is closer to the NVZ takes the middle ball. Eliminates the "stare at partner" momentum killer.
