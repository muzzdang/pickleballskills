# NEW 2026 Pickleball Rule Changes Explained

**Source type:** YouTube video

## Key Takeaways

- **Net post clarification:** The ball is now allowed to hit the net post and remain in play as long as it lands in the correct service court; previously this was an automatic fault.
- **Act of volleying timing:** The rule clarifying when the "act of volleying" begins (and thus when NVZ violations can occur) was refined — the act begins when you first move toward the ball with intention to volley, not at the moment of contact.
- **Rally scoring tested:** USA Pickleball continues piloting rally scoring at select events; every rally awards a point regardless of who served, with games to 21 (win by 2). Not yet adopted universally.
- **Serve rules tightened:** Stricter enforcement on serve motion — arm must move in an upward arc at contact, and the highest point of the paddle head must not be above the wrist at contact. Spin serves via finger manipulation remain banned.
- **Extra ball / timeout rules:** Players may now call a timeout before the score is called; once the score is announced the rally has effectively begun. Using an extra ball from pocket is a fault in rally play.
