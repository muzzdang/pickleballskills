# Anna Leigh's Lob Serve is Changing Pickleball

## Key Takeaways

- **Lob serve mechanics:** Paddle face points toward the side fence at setup; swing down-out-up with an open paddle face at contact; brush back toward the body (not through the ball) ending in a "flex" position. The brushing motion generates backspin and height simultaneously.
- **Depth is everything:** The serve must land in the last 2–3 feet of the baseline to be effective. Any shorter and opponents can step in, attack off the bounce, and rush the net — leaving the serving team in worse shape than a normal serve.
- **Defensive-to-offensive conversion:** Serving is normally a defensive position; the lob serve flips this by forcing the return team deep, delaying their approach to the kitchen by several seconds, and generating a weak pop-up return the server can attack.
- **Odd-side targeting:** Anna Leigh uses this serve far more from odd (left) side to odd (left) side — the serve trajectory naturally targets a right-handed opponent's backhand, which is harder to attack and pulls them off the court. Even-side to even-side is less effective because the trajectory favors the opponent's forehand.
- **Stacking exploit:** The lob serve is most lethal when opponents are stacking, because the player returning must travel back to baseline AND then re-swap positions before the next shot, causing severe late arrival at the kitchen.
- **Counter: take it off the bounce:** The best return against a lob serve is to step in and take the ball at the rise (just after the bounce) at waist height, staying in the court rather than letting it push you to the back fence.
