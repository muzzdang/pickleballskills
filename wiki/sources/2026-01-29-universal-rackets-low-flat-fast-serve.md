## Key Takeaways

- The flat/fast serve is distinct from the topspin serve — lower trajectory, less spin, more pace
- Key challenge: keeping it low enough to clear the net with minimal margin, without faulting
- Contact point is likely higher than the topspin serve — driving through the ball rather than brushing up
- The "step-by-step" framing suggests specific checkpoints: stance, toss height, contact zone, swing speed
- Flat serves are harder to attack from the return side because pace reduces reaction time
- Trade-off vs. topspin: less consistent but more difficult to handle when placed wide or at the body
