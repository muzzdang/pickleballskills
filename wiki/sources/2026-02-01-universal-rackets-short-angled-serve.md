# How to Hit a Short, Angled Pickleball Serve (Universal Rackets)

**Source:** YouTube
**Channel:** [Universal Rackets](../entities/universal-rackets.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fvy7ZdZuPqk
**Duration:** 14:13
**Uploaded:** 2026-02-01

## Key Takeaways

- **Angle creates angle: stand wide to serve wide.** Stand at the very edge of the service box, body facing the wide target. It's very difficult to serve a sharp angle from the center — standing wide makes the geometry easier. Opponents can't read this from center because they've been seeing deep serves from the same position.
- **Face the target for this serve (opposite of normal).** Normal serve: face away from target to enable rotation → power. Short angled serve: face toward the target — you don't need power, you need placement accuracy. "Wherever you point your body, the ball goes."
- **The topspin dink progression.** To learn this serve: (1) Stand at kitchen, hit topspin forehand dinks crosscourt, feeling how to brush up on the ball; (2) Step back one foot at a time, doing the same brush motion; (3) Hit the serve from the baseline, aiming to land in the kitchen. If it lands in the kitchen, you're swinging too much upward — add more forward. If it goes long, brush up more.
- **Swing upward, not forward — "carving the ball."** Getting less of the ball (brushing the back) creates the spin that makes the ball curve and drop into the kitchen. Think of a beanbag toss: soft, accurate, not muscled.
- **Toss slightly more to the outside (2:00 or 3:00).** For the short angled serve, toss around 2-3 o'clock instead of the normal 1:00 — this positions the ball on the outside of your swing path, allowing you to get around and under it to create sidespin.
- **Use case: when up in the score, when opponent is standing far back.** Pull them wide, then hit your next shot up the middle — their partner won't know who covers it. Or follow the wide serve by aiming the next shot down the T.
- **Commitment is required.** A half-committed short serve produces a lofty floater to the kitchen — the worst possible outcome. Go for it fully or don't hit it.
- **Michele's observation:** the feel is like a drop — tossing a beanbag into a hole for accuracy, not power.

## Visual Notes

Michele hit the serve correctly within two attempts on camera after the progressions — Tyler credits the drill sequence. Key visual: her body facing the wide target, toss shifted to 2-3 o'clock, ball curving down into the service box.
