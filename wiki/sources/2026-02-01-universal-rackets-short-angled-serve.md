## Key Takeaways

- The short angled serve pulls the receiver far off the court, creating an open lane for the third shot
- Technique requires specific contact: slice or sidespin creates the angle and keeps the bounce low
- Critically, it must land close to the sideline and the kitchen line — a short serve that doesn't angle wide is easy to attack
- Receiver pulled wide means their return must travel diagonally across a longer court — more time for the serving team
- Counter-strategy: practiced returners can angle their return cross-court to open up the server's side
- Connects to [court angles](../concepts/court-angles.md) — this is an aggressive serve designed to open the court
