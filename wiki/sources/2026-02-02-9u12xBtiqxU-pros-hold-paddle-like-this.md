# Pickleball Pros Hold Their Paddle Like This — You Should Too

**Source type:** YouTube video

**Uploaded:** 2026-02-02

## Key Takeaways

- **Zone-based grip position:** Hang off the end of the handle at the baseline (for power and reach); choke up toward the paddle throat as you advance toward the kitchen (for hand speed and control). This is a deliberate zone-by-zone adjustment, not a fixed grip.
- **Hybrid grip:** Use a continental/eastern hybrid — not a full western or true continental. This allows efficient transitions between forehand and backhand dinks without re-gripping.
- **Midcourt transition grip shift:** As you cross through the transition zone (mid-court / no-man's-land), actively slide your hand up the handle. By the time you reach the kitchen line, your hand should be choked up. Ben Johns and Gabe Tardio both demonstrate this clearly on film.
- **Tungsten tape tip:** Adding tungsten tape to the head of the paddle shifts weight forward and increases swing weight without changing handle feel — useful for players who need more power from a lighter paddle.
- **Choke up = faster hands:** The shorter lever arm created by choking up reduces the time needed to swing the paddle, making hand exchanges at the net faster and more controlled.
