## Key Takeaways

- The toss is the most underrated element of the serve — a poor toss forces compensation and creates inconsistency
- Key toss principles: release the ball from below the waist (per pickleball rules), consistent height, out in front of the strike zone
- Toss height: too high creates timing issues and gives wind more time to move the ball; too low doesn't give swing time
- Toss direction: if tossing slightly forward of vertical, you naturally contact the ball out in front — which generates better power
- The toss must be repeatable — random toss height is the number one cause of missed serves
- Drill: practice the toss alone (without swinging) until the drop-point is consistent each time
