## Key Takeaways

- "Be aggressive and put away more balls" — teaches players when and how to attack, not just what to attack
- Primary focus: the [attack trigger](../concepts/attack-trigger.md) — only attack when ball is above net height
- Likely covers the mechanics of putting away balls: paddle path (down and angled), contact (out in front), wrist snap for pace
- Common mistake: swinging level or upward on a put-away, sending the ball long
- Speed-up mechanics: directed at the opponent's shoulder/body with a compact motion — not a wild swing
- Closing thought: offense is earned through patience, not forced through aggression
