# Have Faster Hands than 95% of Players by Doing This

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rKxrqsBLIA8
**Uploaded:** 2026-02-23

## Key Takeaways

- **FAST framework:** Fully ready (reset paddle to ready position after every shot), Aim (pick a target before the ball arrives), Shift (use the box step to position body behind the ball), Train (dedicated hands-battle drilling is required — can't be built through games alone).
- **Box step footwork:** Instead of crossing feet or lunging, move in a box pattern — small steps that keep the base wide and weight centered. This preserves balance and allows immediate directional change when the ball is redirected.
- **Hayden Patriquin body mechanics breakdown:** Austin analyzes Hayden's hands-battle footage — elbows out and away from body (not tucked), "sandwich" paddle position on forehand volleys (paddle face closed slightly, elbow drives contact), "eagle" position on backhand (arm extends like a wing, contact out in front). These positions create a larger effective reach without lunging.
- **Elbow position is the key variable:** Players who lose hands battles almost always have elbows tucked into the body, shortening reach and reducing reaction time. Moving elbows out is the single biggest correction.
- **Choke up at the kitchen:** Reinforces the zone-based grip concept — at the kitchen, choked-up grip shortens the lever arm and makes hand exchange faster (less paddle travel per swing).
