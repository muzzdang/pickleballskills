# Ben Johns 10 Simple Rules to Win — Copy His Strategy

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LnlFWd5JvEw
**Uploaded:** 2026-03-02

## Key Takeaways

- **Bait ball:** Ben deliberately hits soft balls to specific zones to invite opponents to attack into a predictable location, then has his counter-attack pre-loaded. He "manufactures" the opportunity rather than waiting for it.
- **String theory for partner movement:** Ben and his partner move as if connected by a string — when one player shifts left, the other mirrors right. This eliminates gaps in the middle and prevents opponents from exploiting the seam.
- **Ten rules summary:** (1) Always cover the middle; (2) Hit to the player in transition; (3) Use the bait ball; (4) Take every attackable ball; (5) Reset when off-balance; (6) Keep depth on drives; (7) Partner mirrors your movement; (8) Attack the body in hands battles; (9) Move forward after every drop; (10) Serve deep, not just in.
- **Middle strategy to beat Ben:** Austin notes that Hayden Patriquin and Anna Bright defeated Ben Johns and Anna Leigh Waters in a notable match primarily by hitting every ball up the middle — exploiting communication gaps and taking away Ben's angles.
- **Body attack rule:** Ben's rule 8 — in hands battles, attack the opponent's body (hip or shoulder) rather than aiming for open court. Body balls force awkward mechanics and increase forced errors.
