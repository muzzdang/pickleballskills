# 3 Strategies That Make Hayden Patriquin So Hard to Beat

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9RpG7bZvgX0
**Uploaded:** 2026-03-09

## Key Takeaways

- **Hayden's ready position:** Elbows out and away from the body, paddle slightly open, weight slightly forward on the balls of the feet. He maintains this reset position after every single shot — never allowing the paddle to drift low or elbows to tuck in.
- **Hit middle against Ben Johns:** Hayden's primary tactic against Ben is to eliminate angles by targeting every ball up the middle seam. This exploits communication uncertainty between partners and removes Ben's ability to step around for an inside-out forehand.
- **Cover the middle with forehand:** Hayden takes the middle with his forehand whenever possible — even balls that appear to be his partner's ball. His forehand is powerful enough that poaching the middle is a net positive even accounting for partner disruption.
- **Body-bag attack:** Hayden deliberately targets opponents' bodies (hip, shoulder, torso) rather than open court during hands battles. Balls at the body are the hardest to deflect cleanly. This is a deliberate pattern, not random.
- **Anna Leigh Waters vulnerability:** Austin notes that Anna Leigh's two-handed backhand has a vulnerability at the body — balls directed at her right hip force an awkward two-handed swing with the elbow in an inefficient position. Hayden exploits this pattern.
