# Pickleball Just Changed Forever (17-Foot Court)

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=il4BsDQQfDU
**Uploaded:** 2026-03-10

## Key Takeaways

- **17-foot singles court:** USA Pickleball / PPA Challenger level implemented a shortened singles court — 17 feet wide instead of the standard 20 feet (same dimensions as a standard badminton singles court). Doubles court width remains unchanged.
- **Drops viable again in singles:** On the standard 20-foot court, singles players could attack most drops because of the open court and time to step in. On the 17-foot court the reduced width creates more cat-and-mouse play near the kitchen, making drops and resets meaningful again.
- **Same as badminton dimensions:** Austin observes that the 17-foot width matches a standard badminton singles court exactly — providing a ready-made template that venues already have lined on courts.
- **More strategic depth:** Austin's live review of 17-foot singles play shows longer rallies, more dink exchanges, and fewer outright winners — the court reduction rewards consistency and placement over power.
- **Currently PPA Challenger level only:** As of March 2026, the 17-foot court is being tested at the PPA Challenger (developmental/amateur) level, not yet at the Tour (professional) level. Austin expects broader adoption if early results are positive.
