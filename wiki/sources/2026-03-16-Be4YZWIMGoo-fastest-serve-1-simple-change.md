# This is the Fastest Serve in Pickleball — 1 Simple Change

**Source type:** YouTube video

## Key Takeaways

- **Seven mechanics for maximum serve speed:** (1) Bounce the ball before tossing (gets arm rhythm started); (2) Pull elbow back during the wind-up; (3) C-shaped swing arc (elbow leads, then paddle head whips through); (4) Elbow drives inward at contact (generates pronation); (5) Stand tall / straighten legs at contact (adds height); (6) Full shoulder rotation — non-paddle shoulder drives through; (7) Jump or push off back foot immediately after contact to begin forward movement.
- **The "slap" mindset:** Austin instructs players to think of the serve motion as slapping the ball rather than pushing it. This mental cue activates faster wrist snap and more natural pronation.
- **C-motion is the key mechanical change:** Most recreational servers use a flat pendulum swing. The C-motion — where the elbow leads backward then the paddle head whips in a curve — is the single mechanic that generates the biggest speed increase, hence the "1 simple change" framing.
- **Stand at contact:** Bending knees on the wind-up is fine, but straightening to full height at the moment of contact raises the contact point and adds a vertical force component to the serve.
- **Follow-through drives forward momentum:** Players who complete the follow-through naturally push off the back foot, giving them a head start toward the kitchen — turning serve mechanics into transition mechanics simultaneously.
