## Key Takeaways

- Covers fast-hands volley exchanges and the defensive response when attacked — a complete net-game system
- Forehand volley: compact punch, elbow in front, contact point ahead of the body, wrist firm at contact
- Backhand volley: trickier — elbow tucks, face angle is critical, less wrist action than forehand
- Fast-hands scenarios: the ball is coming fast; no time for a swing — the volley becomes a redirect using the paddle face
- "What to do when attacked" section: absorb and redirect using [reset mechanics](../concepts/reset-mechanics.md), or block to the attacker's feet
- A key concept: the volley is a placement shot, not a power shot — the pace is already provided by the opponent
