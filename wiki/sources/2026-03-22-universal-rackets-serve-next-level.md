# Take Your Pickleball Serve to the Next Level (Universal Rackets)

## Key Takeaways

Focused on the most common reason good players still have mediocre serves: they don't commit to their swing:

- **Tip 1: Commit — swing twice as fast.** The number one serve improvement at all levels: players slow their swing down trying to get the ball in. This is fine when learning, but it's the ceiling. "Double your swing speed" is the cue. "To gain control, give up control. Let the paddle work for you." Go to practice and swing as hard as you can — don't care where it goes. Just commit.
- **Commitment confirmation: elbow points to target, OR touch your back.** (1) Point the elbow toward the center of the box after follow-through — confirms full swing and also aids accuracy. (2) Touch the back of your body with the paddle after contact — same commitment verification. Either works, use whichever resonates.
- **Tip 2: Face the dominant-side net post.** Right-handed player = face the right net post. This is the rotation-enabling body position. Without it, players face the target and use only their arm. With it, they can rotate hips + shoulders.
- **Tip 3: Hips sideways before contact, forward after.** Hips sideways at setup → hips forward at finish. This is the rotation sequence. Think: hips sideways, hips forward. Rotation + acceleration = serve power.
- **Tip 4: Bend and load the legs; explode into the serve.** Don't just stand straight and arm the ball. Get into the legs, drive through the ground upward. The legs connect the ground force to the paddle.
- **Tip 5: Get under the ball and swing up.** Low to high. Not straight ahead. This creates topspin and net clearance.
- **Tip 6: Play with height.** Aim above the net — don't try to skin the net. Height + topspin = ball still goes deep and kicks. Playing with height is the margin that allows full commitment without constant faulting.

## Visual Notes

Tyler demonstrates full-commitment serve — showing the contrast between the restrained serve (paddle barely moves, ball loops weakly) and the committed serve (full arc, ball drives deep with pace). Elbow pointing toward center of box shown as the commitment verification pose.
