## Key Takeaways

- The down-the-line shot is a powerful weapon when deployed at the right time — but often over-used and easy to defend if predictable
- Strategy: the down-the-line shot opens up when you've established the cross-court rally first — consistent with [court angles](../concepts/court-angles.md) principle
- Technique: the body must be aligned toward the target line; the face of the paddle directs the ball — a common error is trying to guide the ball down the line with a cross-court body position
- Footwork component: stepping into the shot with the inside foot positions the hip correctly for a natural line drive
- When to use: when the opponent has shifted cross-court to cover your preferred angle, leaving the line open
- Covers both forehand and backhand down-the-line shots — the backhand version is often neglected but tactically valuable
