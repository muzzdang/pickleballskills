# How to Hit the Down the Line Shot (Universal Rackets)

## Key Takeaways

Tyler and Michele emphasize both the technique and strategic context of the DTL shot — a weapon specifically for the less-dominant side player:

- **Why hit DTL: to burn the poacher.** Michele's use case: in mixed doubles, when the male player (typically stronger) is poaching the middle, go DTL to punish them. Also: don't float to the middle where a right-handed player gets their forehand — burn them up the line instead.
- **The contact point secret: wait a split second longer.** Cross-court happens when you make contact early (out front, across your body). Down-the-line happens when you let the ball get 2 inches closer — now your swing goes more FORWARD rather than ACROSS your body. The contact point difference is small but significant. "Two inches closer, and now I'm swinging forward."
- **Keep the body sideways.** Prematurely opening the body toward the target makes the paddle follow — you'll pull the shot crosscourt even if you were trying for DTL. Stay turned, swing forward.
- **"Wherever you point your body, the paddle follows."** This is the core principle for accuracy in ALL shots at Universal Rackets, applied here to explain why the body must stay sideways for DTL.
- **After the DTL shot: follow the ball.** You went down the line → your body follows that direction → your partner covers the middle (which opened up). Same "follow the ball" principle from the bad habits video.
- **The less-dominant side player context.** When standing on the right side (ad court for righties), your forehand is to the middle — your partner takes those. Your weapon from that side is the DTL backhand. This is why DTL mastery matters especially for the right-side player in doubles.
- **DTL dink: same principle.** DTL dinks are underused — most players only dink crosscourt. DTL dink from the right side goes directly at the opponent standing near the left sideline.

## Visual Notes

Tyler demonstrates early vs. late contact points — paddle arc showing crosscourt vs. forward swing path. Body stays sideways through the DTL in slow demo. Michele shows the follow-the-ball movement after a DTL shot — she moves to the line while Tyler covers middle.
