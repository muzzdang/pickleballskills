# If I Wanted to Add +1.0 to My DUPR in 2026 — Drills Part 1

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tVtqK2dxSdc
**Uploaded:** 2026-03-30

## Key Takeaways

- **Guest coach Kieran:** A D1 tennis player and certified strength & conditioning coach who also runs the [Dinkcredibles](../entities/dinkcredibles.md) channel. His expertise is marrying athletic movement science with pickleball mechanics.
- **Three foundational dink exercises:** (1) Dink tosses — partner tosses balls softly while you practice the contact and follow-through without a paddle, training arm path; (2) Paddle half-hits — use only the top half of the paddle face for dinks to train centered contact; (3) Scoop and catch — swing through the dink motion and catch the ball in your non-dominant hand to feel the finish position.
- **Legs = springs, arm = ribbon:** Kieran's cue system — legs should absorb and generate energy (like springs compressing and releasing); the arm should remain loose and "ribbony" through the dink, never rigid. Tension in the arm is the primary cause of shanked dinks.
- **Semi-open stance:** For dink mechanics, Kieran advocates a semi-open stance (feet at 45 degrees to the net) rather than fully sideways. This loads the hips and allows shoulder rotation to drive the swing rather than relying on arm-only mechanics.
- **Athletic base first:** Before any paddle-specific drill, Kieran emphasizes establishing a wide, athletic stance at the kitchen — knees bent, weight forward, butt low. This is the prerequisite for every dink mechanic he teaches.
