# If I Wanted to Add +1.0 to My DUPR in 2026 — Drills Part 2

**Source type:** YouTube video

**Uploaded:** 2026-04-02

## Key Takeaways

- **Cooperative → competitive drill progression:** Kieran's core methodology — start fully cooperative (both players helping each other succeed), then cooperative-competitive (one player competes while one feeds cooperatively), then fully competitive (normal game play). Skipping levels causes players to default to bad habits under pressure.
- **Skinny singles cooperative:** Two players rally crosscourt on half the court — 100 consecutive dinks is the target before moving to competitive format. The reduced court width forces precise placement and eliminates the impulse to attack.
- **Cooperative-competitive transition drill:** One player feeds balls through the transition zone cooperatively; the other must make 5 clean transitions from baseline to kitchen before the format flips. Trains the transition shot in isolation before combining with full rally.
- **Bosnian volleys:** A specific volley drill Kieran demonstrates — players volley at half speed with an exaggerated open paddle face to feel proper contact angle. Named after the drill's origin in Eastern European tennis training.
- **Dink game to 3:** Short competitive sets (first to 3 points, dink-only) played between drilling blocks. The short format keeps intensity high, the competitive element transfers drilling mechanics to game situations, and the quick reset allows many repetitions.
