# 5 Rules Ben Johns & Anna Leigh Waters Use to ALWAYS WIN

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gBGRF6IzKQg
**Uploaded:** 2026-04-06

## Key Takeaways

- **Rule 1 — Depth on return and 4th shot:** Both the return and the 4th shot must land deep (within 3 feet of the baseline). A shallow return or 4th shot gives the opposing team time to advance and neutralizes the advantage of being at the kitchen.
- **Rule 2 — Cover the middle always:** When in doubt, Ben and Anna Leigh both move toward the middle rather than covering the sideline. The middle is the highest-probability target for opponents and the most costly if unguarded.
- **Rule 3 — One-up one-back positioning:** When a partner is pulled wide or back, the other player moves up to cover the middle — never both players back and never both players forward unless both have arrived at the kitchen simultaneously.
- **Rule 4 — Cut when reaching, topspin when in position:** If you are reaching or off-balance, slice/cut the ball (puts the ball low with backspin, buying time). If you are set and balanced, drive with topspin. Never try to attack from a reaching position.
- **Rule 5 — Put aways go through the middle:** When putting away a high ball, aim for the middle of the court (between the two opponents) rather than the sideline. The middle is harder to defend, requires a communication decision between partners, and a mis-hit to the sideline goes out. The "ATP corridor" through the middle is the pro standard.
