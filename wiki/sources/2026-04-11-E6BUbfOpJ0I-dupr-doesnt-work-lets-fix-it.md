# DUPR DOESN'T WORK. Let's Fix It!

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=E6BUbfOpJ0I
**Uploaded:** 2026-04-11

## Key Takeaways

- **Austin's personal example:** Austin won a 5.0-rated tournament with a 5.3 DUPR — and his rating went DOWN to 5.2 afterward. The system penalized him for competing in a below-rated bracket.
- **Combined ratings problem:** DUPR calculates doubles ratings by averaging partners' ratings, which means two 4.0 players are rated the same combined as a 5.0 and a 3.0 — a fundamentally flawed equivalence, because the 5.0/3.0 pair will consistently lose to the 4.0/4.0 pair.
- **Score differential distortion:** DUPR weights score differential (how many points you won or lost by) in its algorithm. This incentivizes running up scores against weaker opponents and creates perverse incentives in bracket play.
- **Wrong bracket problem:** The system can place higher-rated players in lower brackets (through technicalities or registration issues), and winning that bracket actively hurts their rating.
- **Austin's alternative:** Austin announced he is developing an alternative rating system designed around actual match outcomes (win/loss only, no score differential), partner-independent individual ratings, and bracket-quality normalization. No launch date given.
