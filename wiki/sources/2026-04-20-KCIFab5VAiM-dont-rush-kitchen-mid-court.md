# Don't Rush the Kitchen. Master the Mid Court in 3 Steps

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KCIFab5VAiM
**Uploaded:** 2026-04-20

## Key Takeaways

- **Guest coach Mini:** A 5.6-rated player with 20 years of coaching experience. Specializes in mid-court / transition zone mechanics.
- **Step 1 — Wait until invited (MWC):** Monitor-Watch-Crash applied to the mid-court. Do not advance to the kitchen unless the ball invites you forward. An "invitation" = a ball you can hit offensively or a ball that will land short enough that you must move up. Rushing forward without invitation leaves you caught mid-stride.
- **Step 2 — Only attack if you have time (time > height):** Height alone doesn't determine attackability. A high ball that arrives too quickly doesn't give you time to load and attack cleanly. The correct filter is: do I have TIME to execute a quality attack? If yes, attack. If no, reset. Do not confuse a ball being above the net with being attackable.
- **Step 3 — Feet in right position:** Feet must be set (or actively loading from a split-step) BEFORE contact. Wide athletic base, no narrow stance or crossed feet. If your feet aren't set, don't try to attack — reset instead.
- **Compact swing bonus:** Mini's bonus tip for mid-court volleys — dramatically shorten the swing. Full backswing shots go wide or long from the transition zone because the ball arrives faster than at the baseline. A compact, punch-style swing is more reliable.
- **Armpit ball drill:** Mini's signature drill — hold a ball in your armpit during shadow swings to train the elbow-in compact forehand. If the ball drops, your elbow is too far from your body. Forces the punch-volley habit.
