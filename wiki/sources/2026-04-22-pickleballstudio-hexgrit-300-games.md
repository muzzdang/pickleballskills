## Key Takeaways

### HexGrit Durability (300 Games)
- HexGrit surface (used on 11six24 Vapor Power 2 and Hurache Power 2) holds up well through 300 games — significant finding given grit degradation is a common complaint with textured surfaces
- Spin performance remains consistent well into extended use; no dramatic drop-off
- HexGrit is a proprietary raised texture pattern (hexagonal) distinct from standard sandpaper-style grit

### Lead Tape Placement — 300+ Configuration Study
Chris tested over 300 different weight setups across widebody, hybrid, and elongated paddle shapes. Key findings:

**The optimal position for most players:** Bottom corners — centered low on the paddle face, running from the bottom of the neck outward
- 3 inches of 1g tape at bottom corners: +1.28 swing weight, +0.86 twist weight
- 5 inches of 1g tape at bottom corners: +2.18 swing weight, +1.1 twist weight
- Best twist weight gain per swing weight cost of any configuration tested

**Head weight is a swing weight multiplier — use sparingly:**
- 6g at head: +7.37 swing weight
- 12g at head: +14.01 swing weight
- Even small amounts at the tip dramatically increase how hard the paddle is to swing

**Shape-specific rules:**
- **Widebody:** Already stable (high twist weight naturally); benefit more from higher swing weight for ball plow-through — placing weight higher is acceptable
- **Elongated:** Primary candidate for the bottom-corner strategy; already has high swing weight, so maximize twist weight without further increasing swing weight
- **Top corners:** Good compromise when you want both plow-through power and twist weight stability

**Placement zones:**
- **Head/top:** Power + stability but swing weight penalty; hard to swing fast
- **Sides:** Moderate power gain, sweet spot expansion, manageable swing weight cost
- **Throat/lower:** Best stability-per-swing-weight-unit efficiency; minimal hand speed loss
