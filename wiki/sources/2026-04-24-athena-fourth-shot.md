# You're Choosing the Wrong 4th Shot (Here's what to do instead)

**Channel:** Athena Pickleball (Coach Jess)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fD5LwJ8_SmU
**Date:** 2026-04-24
**Duration:** 8:18

## Key Takeaways

- **The 4th shot is underrated** — most players focus on the 3rd shot, but the 4th is where the returning team either holds or loses their offensive advantage. The return of serve gives you the offense; the 4th shot is how you keep it.
- **Three goals of the 4th shot** (in priority order): (1) keep opponents back from the kitchen, (2) apply pressure at their feet, (3) avoid giving them an attackable ball.
- **Four options based on what the 3rd shot gives you** — the decision is read-and-react, not a preset plan:
  1. **Roll volley** — 3rd is mid-level or higher and you've made it to the NVZ; take it out of the air, brush upward with topspin, aim at their feet.
  2. **Strong block** — 3rd is a drive at you; split-step, meet the ball with a firm wall (elbow ~90°, knuckles forward, wrist locked), redirect deep at their feet with equal-and-opposite force.
  3. **Step back and drive** — 3rd is too low to take cleanly out of the air; step back off the ball, turn shoulders fully ("open the door"), drive off the bounce at their feet, then re-advance.
  4. **Dink and neutralize** — 3rd is a great shot and you're reaching or well below the net; concede, soften your hand, dink into the kitchen, let them in, and reset the point.
- **The most common mistake**: staying committed to the kitchen line and taking the ball too low, being forced to hit up into attacking arms. Recognizing when to step back is the key skill.
- **Simple if/then decision framework:**
  - Mid-level or higher → roll volley
  - Ball driven at you → strong block
  - Can't take highest point out of air → step back and drive
  - Reaching / well below net → dink and neutralize

## Visual Notes

- **Roll volley:** Real court footage shows contact well in front of body at waist height, weight forward, paddle extending through the ball. The brushing motion is visible in arm extension.
- **Strong block:** On-screen graphic (green circle) highlights the compact elbow/paddle zone — elbow ~90°, paddle face near-perpendicular, wrist locked. "Knuckles forward" prevents the face from opening and sending the ball up.
- **Step back and drive:** On-screen text overlays read "TURN YOUR BODY" and "TURN YOUR SHOULDERS" — full shoulder rotation into the back leg is the visual cue. She demonstrates with ball in off-hand showing the trophy/loaded position before driving forward.
