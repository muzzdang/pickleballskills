# Why You Can't Get to the Kitchen (Transition Zone Fix)

**Channel:** Cori Elliott
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jNbNDRG38qw
**Date:** 2026-04-24
**Duration:** 7:16
**Format:** CCC (Cori's Coaching Challenge) — live on-court coaching session with student Generosa

## Key Takeaways

- **Advance trigger on drives** — don't go after hitting a drive automatically. Watch the ball leave the paddle; if you can *see* it dipping as it crosses the net, trust it and move in. If you can't read the flight yet, wait.
- **Advance trigger on resets** — don't move forward just because you hit a reset. Only advance when your ball is *meaningfully lower* than the previous exchange. Hitting from the same spot at the same height is correct — wait for an opening. Generosa's error: she moved forward during resets that hadn't improved her position, causing her next shot to sail out.
- **The drop unlocks the advance** — the most reliable path to the kitchen is a quality drop. A consistent drop gives you time and forces the opponent to hit up, creating a ball you can step into. Driving first, advancing early, and hoping for the best is the riskier path.
- **Drop technique: back-to-front, stay low** — weight transfers from back foot to front foot through the swing. Common error: "arming it" — using only the arm with no body. The shoulder drives forward and *down*, not up. Body stays compressed through contact.
- **Foot behind the ball** — get the trail foot directly behind the ball's landing zone. Tennis players slide laterally to the side (wrong for pickleball). The cue: foot behind, paddle in the "pocket" in front.
- **The pocket** — Cori's term for the consistent ready-position contact zone in front of the body. Always return the paddle to the same spot so you're finding the ball reliably rather than reaching or adjusting.

## Visual Notes

- **Frame 15:** Generosa caught frozen in the transition zone — weight already committed forward, leaning toward net but hesitating. Perfect example of the "moved too soon" problem before the ball has earned the advance.
- **Frame 25:** Caption reads "Here we are refining her drop to make it more consistent so she can get to the kitchen more easily." Generosa mid-drop from transition zone — shoulder rotating but still rising slightly through the shot.
- **Frame 35 (Cori's demo — key):** Close-up of correct drop technique. Deep knee bend, weight in legs. Paddle face slightly open, below the wrist. Shoulder drives the motion. Body stays *low* throughout — no rising. Back-to-front lean visible in the stance. This is the corrected version vs. Generosa's arm-only habit.
- **Frame 5:** Generosa's starting stance shows the tennis footwork habit — slightly sideways, not square behind the ball. The lateral slide is what Cori identifies and corrects.
