# Great Power...Lowest Price? Luzz Cannon Review

**Channel:** PickleballPlaybook (Austin Hardy)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CyANMox81Is
**Date:** 2026-04-24
**Duration:** 10:45
**Reviewers:** Austin Hardy + Kieran

## Key Takeaways

- **Verdict in one sentence:** "If you're looking for power in your game, I don't know if there's a harder hitter than this." — but it sacrifices control significantly.
- **Power:** Probably the hardest-hitting Gen 3 paddle available. Shots rocket off the face. Effortless power from the baseline.
- **Spin:** Above average.
- **Control:** Low. Compact motions are mandatory — any wrist or extra swing sends the ball out. Drops and resets require near-perfect mechanics to keep in.
- **Sweet spot:** Smaller than expected for a power paddle. Mishits go completely dead (no trampoline effect). Off-center hits lack feel. Adding lead tape to widen the sweet spot is recommended.
- **Sound:** Very loud, cannon-like "donk." Audibly one of the most distinctive-sounding paddles.
- **Break-in period:** Significant. Both reviewers hit many balls long in the first 10 minutes; it became more manageable after break-in.
- **Price:** ~$99 retail / ~$92.65 with code PLAYBOOK. One of the cheapest Gen 3 power paddles on the market.
- **Shape:** Elongated.

## Who It's For

- Players who want raw baseline power and don't prioritize control
- Older players who struggle to generate sufficient pace
- ~4.0 male players who win primarily by hitting hard
- Recreational players who play 1-2x/week and just want to blast the ball

## Who Should Avoid It

- Kitchen-oriented players or dink-heavy styles
- Anyone who relies on drops, resets, or touch shots
- Players still developing control and consistency
