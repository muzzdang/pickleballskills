# Control Players… You NEED to See This Paddle | Beyond Measure Ronin Review

**Channel:** PickleballPlaybook (Austin Hardy)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i3w8qGG574o
**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Duration:** 10:21
**Reviewers:** Austin Hardy (elongated) + Kieran (hybrid)

## Key Takeaways

- **Verdict:** A control-first, all-court Gen 4 paddle with a massive sweet spot, soft/plush feel, and enough pop to put balls away. Suited to advanced players but accessible to intermediates. Austin says he would personally game this paddle.
- **Power:** Balanced — "nice balanced amount of power." Not a weapon from the baseline, but enough to finish points. Pop is actually higher than raw power suggests: "I can really just stick it here and it really travels."
- **Spin:** T700 carbon fiber face — "exactly like the Luzz Inferno" in terms of spin character; typical T700 output.
- **Control:** Very high. "Way control oriented." Massive sweet spot — reviewers rarely missed during control test. Off-frame hits feel similar to on-face hits (minimal penalty). Called a "marshmallow" paddle.
- **Feel:** Soft, plush, "you feel the ball go into the face and then come out." Significant dwell time. Compared to: bread-and-butter Loco, Friday Aura (but a step above, with more control than Aura Pro).
- **Sweet spot:** "Huge." Only the very tip of the paddle is a dead zone.
- **Handle:** Notably short compared to most paddles — affects two-handed backhand grip users; worth noting before buying.
- **Shapes:** Hybrid (rounder/wider) and Elongated (taller/narrower) — visually distinct, same face material and feel profile.
- **Price:** ~10% off with code PLAYBOOK.
- **Generation:** Gen 4.

## Who It's For

- Control-first players who still want enough pop to finish points
- Advanced players who prioritize kitchen feel over baseline power
- Intermediate/beginner players who want a forgiving, soft paddle to develop touch
- Players who like the Friday Aura feel but want more control

## Who Should Look Elsewhere

- Power-first players or baseline bangers
- Players who need a long handle for two-handed backhands

## Comparisons

| Paddle | Relationship |
|---|---|
| Friday Aura | Very similar feel; Ronin is a step above with more control |
| Aura Pro | Ronin sits between Aura and Aura Pro — more control than Pro, more advanced than base Aura |
| Loco (bread and butter) | Similar balanced feel |
| Luzz Inferno | Same T700 spin character, opposite power/control balance |

## Visual Notes

- **Frames 1 & 4:** Both paddles held side by side — hybrid (left, Kieran) is noticeably rounder/wider; elongated (right, Austin) is taller and narrower. Both share dark charcoal/graphite face, teal/cyan accent, "RONIN" in white on face, "BEYOND MEASURE" printed on edge tape.
- **Frame 25:** Kieran at the NVZ with very abbreviated, forward-leaning stroke — the control-paddle technique of extending into shots rather than swinging.
- **Frame 35:** Austin's flick at the NVZ is compact and wrist-led — minimal prep needed even when adding pace.
