# Are Power Paddles Hurting You? (Pickleball Studio)

**Channel:** [Pickleball Studio](../entities/pickleball-studio.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Odro_2MYsKY
**Duration:** ~20 min | **Uploaded:** 2026-04-29

## Key Takeaways

- **Power paddles are not inherently bad** — but they have a real cost for players who haven't built the soft-game foundation to manage them
- **The core argument:** Power doesn't help you if it causes you to pop up dinks, overhit resets, or lose consistency at the kitchen — skills that are developed with control-first paddles
- **Who should avoid power paddles:** Intermediate and developing players (3.0–4.0) who still have inconsistent soft game, dinking errors, or reset problems
- **Who benefits from power paddles:** Players with a solid soft game who want more pace on drives and serves without sacrificing touch — typically 4.0+ with disciplined mechanics
- **Control paddles build better habits:** The feedback from a control paddle (where your errors are) forces technical improvement; power paddles can mask bad mechanics temporarily while reinforcing them long-term
- **Chris's framing:** Choosing a power paddle before you're ready is like putting racing tires on a car you're still learning to drive
