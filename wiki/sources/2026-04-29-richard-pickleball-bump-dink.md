# Stop Slicing Every Dink… BUMP It Instead (More Pressure, More Wins)

**Channel:** Richard Pickleball
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Wd-VFvM4qAA
**Date:** 2026-04-29
**Duration:** 8:44

## Key Takeaways

- **The bump is replacing the slice dink** at higher levels (emerged in the last 6-12 months, driven by new paddle tech). Richard now uses it 50%+ of the time at the NVZ; was at 0% a year ago.
- **What is the bump:** Taking a ball out of the air at the kitchen by pushing forward with the shoulder — paddle pointed forward, face adjusted for ball height, locked wrist. No slice, no roll, no snap. Pure shoulder drive.
- **Why bump instead of slice:**
  1. **More pace** — slice is a dead, decelerating ball. Rollers (two-handed backhand) can read it, back up, and roll aggressively off it. The bump arrives faster and disrupts their timing.
  2. **Counter to the "step-back-and-roll" read** — opponents who read your slice return and back up early to roll off it can't do this against the bump because they don't have time to set up.
  3. **More reach** — slice requires wrist cocked back, limiting forward extension. The bump (paddle pointing forward) gives more reach — useful for pushing toward feet of players not fully at the NVZ.
  4. **Harder to attack** — a bump is harder to speed up than a slice. If someone's crashing the net, bumping past them toward the middle is harder for them to flick than a dead slice.
- **Target zone:** Between the middle of the NVZ box and the middle of the opponent's feet. Stay forward/central; going wide requires closing the face and becomes more like a roll — harder and riskier.
- **When to still slice:** When on the move, out of position, or uncomfortable. The bump requires you to be set.
- **Best used against:** Players who love rolling the two-handed backhand cross-court.

## Technique

1. **Paddle pointed forward** — not angled down like a slice. Face perpendicular to travel direction.
2. **Loosen wrist slightly** — not limp, just a little loose. Then lock it completely once you begin the shot.
3. **Pre-shot wrist hinge only:** Adjust face angle based on ball height before the shot starts:
   - Higher ball → more closed face (paddle tilted slightly down)
   - Lower ball → more open face (paddle tilted up)
4. **All shoulder, no wrist** — once the shot begins, it's shoulder drive only. No snap, no flick.
5. **Push through the ball** — not a wrist shot, not a roll; a controlled forward push that puts pace on without spin.
