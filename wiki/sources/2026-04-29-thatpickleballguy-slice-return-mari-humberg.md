# They Say Don't Slice Returns. This Pro Does Anyway.

**Channel:** ThatPickleballGuy (Kyle Koszuta)
**Guest:** Mari Humberg (pro player, known for heavy slice game)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V6fIzk2Sv1c
**Date:** 2026-04-29
**Duration:** 7:53

## Key Takeaways

- **Why Mari slices returns:** The slice gives more time to get to the kitchen line — the ball decelerates on its way to the server, buying extra transition time. In women's doubles she slices almost 100% of returns.
- **When NOT to slice:** Mixed doubles, against players who hit strong topspin thirds. Spin continuation: a slice coming at them creates topspin when they drive it back — that topspin accelerates in the same direction as their swing, making it easier for them to hit a quality shot. Against flat/slice thirds, the slice return is more effective.
- **Ball type matters:** Franklin balls (lower bounce) make the slice even more effective — server has to get very low to dig it up, and the ball takes longer to fall. Lifetime balls (higher bounce) reduce the advantage.
- **Four teaching points (with on-screen overlays):**
  1. **Split step** — as the server contacts the ball, that's the cue. Split immediately.
  2. **Unit turn** — turn the entire body to the ball side, not just the arm. Whole upper body rotates; hips drive the shoulder.
  3. **Meet the ball** — go to the ball; don't let it come to you. Forward momentum at contact. Still have stillness at the moment of contact, then continue moving forward.
  4. **Contact out in front** — late contact (ball beside or behind body) = paddle face opens = ball pops up. Contact in front = face stays controlled.
- **The slice is not a wrist shot:** No wrist action. Wrist locked. The backspin is generated by the **paddle face angle** and the chopping path — not a flip. "I'm knifing that thing."
- **Attack the ball:** Don't push it. Go after it with commitment. "Counterintuitive: if you stop your body and overuse your arm, you lose control. Go full with the body, let the swing control the outcome."
- **Pace control:** If opponents are hitting quality thirds off it, just slow the shot down — you'll have more time to get up to the kitchen.
