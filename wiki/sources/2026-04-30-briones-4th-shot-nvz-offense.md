# Stop Losing Points on Your 4th Shot (Do This Instead)

**Channel:** Briones Pickleball Academy (Coach Jordan Briones)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PobNqHD1GX8
**Date:** 2026-04-30
**Duration:** 9:33

## Key Takeaways

This video covers the **returning team's 4th shot from the NVZ** — specifically when the returning team is at or near the kitchen and the serving team is transitioning in with drives and drops.

### Core Mindset
Take every ball out of the air. Reasons: (a) takes time away from transitioning opponents, (b) earlier contact = more offensive position. The NVZ is the one position on the court where offense is essential.

### Shot Selection by Contact Height

**Forehand:**
| Ball height | Shot |
|---|---|
| High above net | Swinging volley |
| At net level or slightly below | Roll (vertical, low-to-high motion) |
| Below knee | Step back → attack off bounce at apex |

**Backhand:**
| Ball height | Shot |
|---|---|
| High above net | Full swinging volley (power, keeps ball deep) |
| Around net level / slightly below | Backhand flick (topspin, pace, keeps opponents back) |
| Slightly below net but above knee | Backhand roll (take ball early, topspin, time pressure) |
| Below knee | Step back → attack off bounce with 2H backhand |

**Rule:** Never volley below knee level. Create space, gather feet, attack off the bounce at the apex.

### Scenario 1: Drive-Drop Counter Strategy
Opponents drive the third, then try to drop the fifth. Returning team at NVZ counter-strategy:
1. Hit the deepest, most solid counter you can — but don't overhit and give free points
2. Counters don't all have to be perfect (some will be shorter) — keep them LOW so opponents must drop fifth
3. After a solid counter, lean in and look for swinging volley/flick/roll on the fifth shot
4. If YOU pop up a weak counter ball: don't lean in — hunker down and get ready for another counter. Optionally step back for extra time.

### Scenario 2: Three Options Against Aggressive Topspin Drops
When opponents hit aggressive topspin drops AND crash forward aggressively:

**Option A — Shuffle back + dink angle to sideline:**
Shuffle back, hit a slice or aggressive dink low toward the sideline. Keeps ball away from middle (limits poaching) and away from waist level.

**Option B — Come off line + safe ball to middle:**
Step off the NVZ line, shovel/dink the ball short and low toward the center. Low + middle = limits angles for aggressive opponents wide. Harder for poachers.

**Option C — Half-volley dink (advanced, 5.0+):**
Reach in at last second, decide it's too low to attack, pull back, play safe ball crosscourt or to middle, short into kitchen. Denies aggressive opponents easy attacks.

**Key principle across all three:** Keep balls low, away from middle, away from waist height or above.

### When to Concede the Kitchen Line
If opponents hit a really good third shot that puts you in a defensive position: concede the NVZ line. Step back, absorb, don't try to hold the line and force a shot from a weak position. Better to step back than give them an easy high contact.
