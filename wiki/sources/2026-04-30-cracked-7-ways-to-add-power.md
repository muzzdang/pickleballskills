# 7 Ways to Add Power (Cracked Pickleball)

## Key Takeaways

- [Topspin](../concepts/topspin.md) is the safety net for hard hitting — get under the ball and brush up the backside; without it, power shots sail out
- Power comes from the full [kinetic chain](../concepts/kinetic-chain.md): legs → hips → core → arm → paddle; load the back foot, snap hips closed at contact so chest faces forward
- [Grip position](../concepts/grip-position.md): hold lower on the handle for more power (longer lever = faster paddle head); choke up at the kitchen for faster hands
- Hit the [sweet spot](../concepts/sweet-spot.md): dead center of the paddle face; move your head with the ball all the way through contact to improve consistency
- [Contact point](../concepts/contact-point.md): make contact away from the body, roughly in line with the front knee; active feet are required to get into position
- [Grip pressure](../concepts/grip-pressure.md): loose during the swing, stiffen at impact for a whip-like snap — advanced technique that adds power but also variability
- [Roll shot at the kitchen](../concepts/roll-shot.md): combine [wrist lag](../concepts/wrist-lag.md) with extra-low body position to generate topspin when rolling at the net
