# The Punch Volley Every Player Needs

**Channel:** Cori Elliott
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BiwdbOikhSo
**Date:** 2026-05-01
**Duration:** 7:07
**Format:** CCC — coaching session with student Brooke
**Focus:** Backhand punch volley

## Key Takeaways

- **The punch volley as a weapon, not a reset.** Most players settle for resetting balls they could attack. The backhand punch volley is a compact, locked-wrist attack that keeps pressure without over-swinging.
- **"Door closed, door open":** Start with the paddle parallel to the net (door closed). The swing opens the door — paddle pushes forward, not across. Return to ready position after contact.
- **Lock the wrist.** The single most common error. A loose wrist causes the face to open at contact, sending the ball up or sideways. Once locked, the face stays consistent.
- **Elbow tucked close to the body.** When the elbow drifts high or out, the wrist breaks automatically and the ball goes wide. Elbow in = compact, controlled shot.
- **Compact takeback.** No ball past the shoulder. Start flat and push forward — don't load up a big backswing. "We're at the kitchen already. We don't need to over-swing."
- **Stay square to the net.** Don't turn the body too much sideways to generate power. Power comes from the compact forward push, not body rotation.
- **Lean into the shot.** Don't fall back on heels. Forward weight = better contact and better direction.
- **Paddle tip slightly up, push forward.** A useful drill: push the top of the paddle tip and stop it in front. This gives the feel of the shot without over-committing.
- **Face angle by ball height:**
  - High (chest level or above) → flat/neutral face, push forward
  - Mid-chest/stomach → slight tilt upward on the face, push forward and down toward feet
  - Hip level → more tilt (like the Gabe Tardio style), slightly more rotation
- **When to reset instead:** If the ball is dipping low, reset or hit a flick. The punch volley is for attackable balls — don't force it on balls below comfortable contact height.

## Common Errors (Brooke's session)

| Error | Effect | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Loose wrist | Ball pops up or goes wide | Lock wrist before contact, keep locked through |
| Elbow drifting high/out | Forces wrist to break | Keep elbow close to body |
| Too much body turn | Loses power, ball goes sideways | Stay square; power from compact push, not rotation |
| Big backswing | Loss of control, late contact | Nothing past shoulder; start flat and push |
| Falling back on heels | Weak contact, direction loss | Lean into the shot |
