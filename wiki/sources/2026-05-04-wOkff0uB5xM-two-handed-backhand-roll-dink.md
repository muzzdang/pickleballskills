# Pickleball's Newest Cheat Code — The Two-Handed Backhand Roll Dink

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wOkff0uB5xM
**Uploaded:** 2026-05-04

## Key Takeaways

- **Guest coach Luke Larsen:** A 5.5-rated player with 25 years of experience, specializing in two-handed backhand mechanics.
- **Continental grip on dominant hand:** The two-handed backhand roll dink uses a continental grip on the paddle hand — not an eastern or western. This keeps the paddle face open enough to generate the low-to-high brush that creates topspin.
- **Light grip pressure throughout:** Luke's primary cue — grip the paddle as lightly as possible while still maintaining control. Heavy grip pressure stiffens the wrist and kills the topspin brush. The "broken wrist" feel throughout the swing indicates correct lightness.
- **V-stance contact inside the left knee:** Contact is made with the ball positioned inside the left knee (for right-handed players) — not out in front of the body and not to the side. The "V" shape of the legs frames the contact point. This creates consistent depth and angle.
- **Low-to-high, shoulder-driven:** The swing is driven by the shoulder rotating upward (not the arm pushing forward). The paddle starts below the ball, brushes upward through contact, and finishes at shoulder height. The non-dominant hand provides stability and power through the follow-through.
- **Follow the ball:** Luke's finishing cue — the paddle face should "follow" the ball's path after contact for 6–8 inches before releasing. This extended follow-through prevents the flick/wrist-flip error that sends the ball long.
