# Win More Tournaments With These Drills and Trainings

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L2FImX3dTLI
**Uploaded:** 2026-05-11

## Key Takeaways

- **Skinny singles cooperative (100-dink goal):** Kieran's tournament prep starts with skinny singles cooperative dinking — two players rallying crosscourt on half the court, targeting 100 consecutive dinks before switching to competitive format. The goal is building rhythm and feel, not winning.
- **Cooperative-competitive phase (5 transitions):** One player feeds cooperatively through the transition zone while the other executes 5 clean full-court transitions (baseline to kitchen). Once 5 are completed, the format flips to full competition. Trains the transition shot under a pressure ramp.
- **Fully competitive (to 11):** After cooperative phases, standard game to 11. The transition from cooperative drilling into competitive play transfers mechanics built in isolation into game situations.
- **Pre-tournament relaxation protocol:** Kieran recommends the session the night before a tournament be low-intensity — light ball touching, no hard drilling. Goal is to stay kinetically warm without fatiguing muscles or disrupting sleep.
- **On-court warm-up sequence:** Day-of-tournament warm-up: (1) dynamic movement without paddle (leg swings, lateral shuffles); (2) soft dinking; (3) transition shots; (4) full-pace drives and serves. The sequence is deliberately graduated — never start with hard shots cold.
- **Home prep drill:** Kieran's off-court recommendation — shadow footwork with a paddle in front of a mirror, 5 minutes daily. Builds the neuromuscular pattern for split-step, box step, and kitchen approach without needing a court.
