# The Secret to Winning Pickleball: Stop Trying to Hit Winners

**Type:** YouTube instructional
**Date:** 2026-05-14
**Channel:** [Briones Pickleball Academy](../entities/briones-pickleball-academy.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WdCSjucEQ-s
**Duration:** 8:20

## Key Takeaways

- **Unforced errors outnumber winners** at all levels including pro — stop chasing outright winners and apply pressure instead; let opponents make mistakes
- **Third-shot drive: shorten the backswing** — a big backswing forces full acceleration and kills control; limited backswing + loose grip + set feet = dipping, consistent drive around 65% power
- **Kitchen-line overhead trap**: when a ball arrives at shoulder height near the net, the amateur instinct is to snap an overhead wrist-down, which leads to net errors; instead slide sideways and hit a sweeping volley (swing through and across, not down) or preset the paddle face and push through with the hand leading, not the paddle tip
- **First speed-up**: avoid the big windup — use a shuffle step to create space, drop the paddle (not take it back), then brush up the back of the ball with topspin to keep control; the goal of the first speed-up is to set up a better second ball, not to end the point immediately
- **Every ball is a setup** — if you're not in a dominant position, play for the next ball, not the winner

## Visual Notes

Demonstrates short vs. long backswing on the third-shot drive side by side. The correct version shows a compact unit turn, loose arm, and early foot prep — the drive dips at the returner's feet rather than going long. Speed-up drill shows the paddle drop rather than backswing as the prep move.
