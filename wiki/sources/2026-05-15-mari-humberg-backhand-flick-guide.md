# The Mari Humberg Backhand Flick Guide (FINALLY)

**Type:** YouTube instructional
**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Channel:** [Mari Humberg](../entities/mari-humberg.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xTgBWGCTa0k
**Duration:** 8:42

## Key Takeaways

- **Flick vs. roll distinction**: the flick is wrist-driven (windshield-wiper motion); the roll is shoulder-driven — they are different shots requiring different mechanics and situations; this video covers only the flick
- **Grip**: continental or between continental and eastern; Mari has recently started choking up but says this is not required — the flick works from any grip position along the handle
- **The teaching drill**: stand at the net, place the paddle against it, and flick upward — no forward force, pure rotation; this ingrains the low-to-high windshield-wiper path that generates topspin and gets the ball over the net
- **When to go for it**: ball between knee and hip height; open stance with shoulder turned (unit turn); contact far out in front of the body with elbow extended (not bent); if the ball is below the knee, do not attempt the flick
- **Four target zones from the left side**: (1) down the line to opponent's right hip, (2) down the middle to opponent's left shoulder or facing player's right shoulder, (3) full crosscourt at opponent's body, (4) sharp short-angle crosscourt — the short angle is the hardest to execute but is a clean winner if the ball is slightly high

## Visual Notes

Slow-motion footage shows the wrist path: paddle starts low, rotates upward through the ball like a windshield wiper. The contact happens with elbow extended and arm reaching forward — not a compact motion. Occasionally Mari's wrist curls behind the hand; she notes this is not intentional but the low-to-high rotation is still the key. The open stance with shoulder unit turn is clearly visible; she does not pivot fully closed.
