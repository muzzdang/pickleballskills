# PaddleTek Honeyfoam TKO-X 16mm First Impressions

**Type:** YouTube paddle review
**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Channel:** [Pickleball Studio](../entities/pickleball-studio.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FdFMP24Pgb4
**Duration:** 8:15

## Key Takeaways

- **Yola lawsuit context**: the Paddletek Honeyfoam was named in the Yola Gen 3 design lawsuit; Paddletek settled, will pay royalties, and must stop selling the paddle in fall 2026 — buy multiples if you love it
- **Identical to Paddletek Reserve**: the Reserve was the limited-edition early-access version; the Honeyfoam is the mass-market release with no changes; Reserve owners need not repurchase
- **Core and specs**: EPP foam center surrounded by a narrow horseshoe EVA ring (narrower than most foam paddles); elongated shape only (for now); swing weight ~118; 5.75 in handle; 4.25 in grip (blocky — not ideal for smaller hands); 16mm is USAPA+UPAA dual certified, 14mm is UPAA-only
- **Feel profile**: soft and dense — more muted and dampened than stiff-hollow paddles; described as sitting between "control" and "low-tier power"; dynamic pop profile where swing speed determines output more than usual
- **Compared to Friday Aura**: the Aura is half the price (~$129 vs $250), plays in a very similar soft-and-dense realm but with less pop; Honeyfoam has more power ceiling but no longer-lasting grit; Aura may be the smarter buy unless you specifically need the extra pop
- **Honest verdict**: good paddle in an underserved market niche (soft-dense-power), but overpriced without a durable grit texture; sweet spot slightly below average for an elongated, improvable with corner weight; a meaningful upgrade over the old Bantam
