# The OFF BALL Secret to Winning Firefights in Pickleball

## Key Takeaways

- **Part 3 of Richard's off-ball player series** — focuses on what the non-involved player does during a partner-vs-opponent firefight
- **Paddle tip points at the opponent in the firefight** — not at the ball, not in a lazy neutral position; this single adjustment allows quick sliding to backhand or forehand without any wasted movement; "if you're getting hit with redirected balls, your paddle is in the wrong spot"
- **Up-the-line firefight (most common)**: off-ball player keeps paddle pointed toward the firefighting opponent and takes a half-step toward the middle to cut off redirect angles; body stays engaged, weight forward — never flat-footed watching the battle
- **Cross-court firefight (left-left matchup)**: when partner attacks or gets attacked crosscourt, the right-side player "packs the middle" — creeps toward center court so any redirect that comes across can be cleaned up; the sideline is conceded, the middle becomes a wall
- **Packing the middle**: with both players holding backhand in the middle, the forehand corridor is effectively closed — this is a pattern Richard identifies as elite-level doubles positioning; righty plays 2-backhand, 1-forehand; lefty can mirror or stay neutral

## Visual Notes

Richard demonstrates paddle position clearly: he shows the "lazy" position (paddle hanging to the side) vs. the engaged position (tip pointing at opponent). During live firefight demonstrations, the camera focuses on Richard's off-ball movement — visible half-step creep toward the middle and constant weight-forward engagement, never standing still.
