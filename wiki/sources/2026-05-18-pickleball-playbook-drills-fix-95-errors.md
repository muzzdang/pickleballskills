# These Drills Fix 95% of Errors in Pickleball

**Type:** YouTube instructional
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Channel:** [PickleballPlaybook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UjdZwT8u4xY
**Duration:** 14:32

## Key Takeaways

- **Consistency is the game-changing skill** — the team that wins is the team hitting every dink, drop, and reset consistently; flashy shots (Ernie, ATP) are not what win championships; this applies at every level from 3.5 to pro
- **Learning priority order**: control → consistency → location → power; adding power before the first three creates chaos; players get this sequence backwards by chasing power paddles
- **4 drills, in sequence** —
  1. **Drop Consistency Game**: play to 11; the player at the baseline hits drops, the net player attacks; if the dropper misses they swap positions; only score points when you're the dropper; forces reps under pressure across all four court positions
  2. **Reset Consistency Game**: same game-to-11 format but from the midcourt — you must stay in the transition zone; forces comfort in the most uncomfortable position on court
  3. **Volley Consistency Drill**: both players at midcourt hit cooperative volleys to 50 in a row; pressure builds as you approach the goal; reset to zero on any miss
  4. **Dink Shallow Game**: cones mark the first 3/4 of the kitchen (closest to the net); dinks outside the zone score a point for the opponent; you can also score by taking balls out of the air before they bounce — rewards offensive volley dinking while punishing deep floaters
- **Ground push / ground force concept**: rate of foot-push into the floor determines arm speed which determines ball distance; slow push = dink, medium = drop, fast = drive — useful cue for controlling depth without thinking about arm mechanics
