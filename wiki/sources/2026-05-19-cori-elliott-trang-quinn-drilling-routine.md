# How the #1 Viet Pro Trains (Full 5.89 DUPR Drilling Routine)

**Type:** YouTube instructional / drilling footage
**Date:** 2026-05-19
**Channel:** [Cori Elliott](../entities/cori-elliott.md)

**Duration:** 12:27

## Key Takeaways

- **Subject**: Trang Quinn, described as the #1 Vietnamese professional pickleball player at a 5.89 DUPR rating; drills with Cori Elliott at high competitive speed
- **Trang's drilling structure**: straight dinking → cross-court dinking → reset game → drops and drives → cooperative volleys → speed-up game → combo pattern drill; this sequence from kitchen work outward mirrors the recommended order in multiple instructional sources
- **Reset game drill**: Trang's version — the player off the kitchen must get the ball to bounce in the kitchen 5 consecutive times while the net player tries to prevent it; forces soft hands and patience under constant pressure; not about winning the point, about executing the skill
- **Drilling philosophy**: Trang emphasizes timed drilling sessions ("go for each ball for a set time") over score-keeping — keeps focus on patterns and quality of reps, not results; she calls out working specifically on her "off the bounce speed-up and follow-through with the second shot"
- **Speed-up game structure**: one player initiates speed-ups, the other works only on counters; then swap; isolates each skill separately rather than mixing randomly

## Visual Notes

Footage is unscripted and casual — shows real high-level drilling at tournament prep intensity. Both players hit topspin dinks with heavy roll; Trang's reset position is notably low with a wide base. Speed-up game shows compact wrist-free redirects consistent with Richard Pickleball's forehand counter mechanics.
