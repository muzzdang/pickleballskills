# The Proton Peacock Just Got a SECOND LIFE | Full Review

**Type:** YouTube paddle review
**Date:** 2026-05-19
**Channel:** [The Kitchen Pickleball](../entities/the-kitchen-pickleball.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DJkdVjqn-NM
**Duration:** 6:40

## Key Takeaways

- **Price dropped dramatically**: launched at $280 (highest tier); Proton restructured pricing, dropping paddles by $100+ depending on shape; stacked with code "kitchen" (15% off) brings price as low as $127 — completely changes the value proposition
- **Specs**: full foam core Gen 4 paddle with Proton's "atomic foam core" center and EVA foam perimeter; carbon fiber face; available in elongated and widebody shapes, each in 13mm and 15mm cores; elongated 15mm reviewed: 8.09 oz static weight, swing weight 118.9, twist weight 6.4
- **Feel**: consistent and well-rounded; "firm but not sharp"; slightly muted/toned-down response that is surprisingly controlled given the thinner-than-standard cores (13mm and 15mm vs. typical 16mm/14mm); sits in the "lower-mid tier of power paddles" — doesn't stand out in any one category but doesn't fail in any either
- **15mm vs 13mm**: 15mm gives more dwell, soft touch, and forgiveness — recommended for most players; 13mm (especially widebody) has a very low swing weight and high twist weight — a specialized weapon for kitchen-heavy, high-hand-speed play
- **Three standout features**: lifetime warranty (rare — reviewer doesn't know any other company offering this), unique paddle shapes (elongated with 5.25 in Yola Magnus-style handle, very square widebody), community-oriented brand (junior PPA tour title sponsor, charity work through Good Sports Foundation, flagship stores in Vietnam)
- **Verdict**: the paddle itself hasn't changed — it was already good at launch; the new pricing makes it a serious consideration; the lifetime warranty adds substantial long-term value
