# Honolulu J2CR LH, PaddleTek HoneyFoam, and Our Top Paddles of 2026

**Type:** YouTube podcast episode
**Date:** 2026-05-20
**Channel:** [Pickleball Studio](../entities/pickleball-studio.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6AxdNnEArxs
**Duration:** 1:20:31

## Key Takeaways

- **Selkirk acquired Bread & Butter**: warranty extended from 6 months to 1 year for all existing paddles — a positive consumer move; sets a higher warranty standard
- **Honolulu J2CR LH (long handle)**: swing weight 108 (lower than the short-handle version — surprising given elongated shape); twist weight 6.1; balance point 231 (very handle-heavy, minimal perimeter weight); Crystal Blue Endurance Grit — UPAA only, not USAPA; early testing shows excellent spin and shot-shaping; weak plow-through out of the box due to low swing weight — needs 3-gram corner weight to perform at full potential; compared favorably to the CR but the grit is the differentiator
- **PaddleTek Honeyfoam vs Friday Aura** (direct head-to-head): Honeyfoam is a low-tier power paddle, Aura is all-court; both are soft-and-dense; Honeyfoam has more pop and better feel for elongated-lovers; Aura is half the price and has a larger sweet spot without adding weight; for players who pop up resets or dinks, go Aura; for players wanting extra juice who don't care about price — Honeyfoam; Aura wins on value
- **Chris's top 2026 paddles (so far)**: Friday Aura Pro (#1 by landslide for price/performance), Spartis P1 (#2 — durable grit, very good all-round), Enhance MPP Turbo series (power at $100 — "insane" value), PaddleTek Honeyfoam (sneaky sleeper pick with right weight setup)
- **Isaac's top 2026 paddles**: Spartis P1, Friday Aura and Aura Pro, Q2 (Boomstick alternative with two thickness options), Enhance MPP Turbo, 11-6-24 Power Two (HexGrit durability — 500+ games still above 2000 RPM), Honolulu J2CR Blue Grit (question mark pending more testing)
- **Key paddle market observations**: spin durability now separating paddles more significantly (P1, 11-6-24 Power Two, Honolulu Blue Grit, Spartis P1 lead); UPAA-only paddles having grit advantage over USAPA-approved; Honolulu perpetually on backorder — 11-6-24 stands out for actually managing inventory well
- **Paddle decision framework primer**: the 12-question primary/secondary system discussed with Chris's primaries (swing weight, paddle feel, Gen 4, shape, power level) and Isaac's primaries (shape, spin, handle length, certification)
