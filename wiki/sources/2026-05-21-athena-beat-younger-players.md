# How to Beat Younger Pickleball Players Without Hitting Harder

**Type:** YouTube instructional
**Date:** 2026-05-21
**Channel:** [Athena Pickleball](../entities/athena-pickleball.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GGKYFOS0jeI
**Duration:** 11:26

## Key Takeaways

- **Context**: Coach Jess is a 48-year-old competing at the open level against players half her age; the video's core message is that physical speed and power are not pickleball's deciding factors — strategy, placement, and patience are
- **Strategy 1 — Make them hit up (target the feet)**: low dinks to the opponent's feet force them to pop the ball up, creating attackable balls; "finding their feet equals attackable balls"; do not speed up randomly off the bounce — build the attack through dink placement; drill: play live points where you can ONLY attack off a pop-up you created through an aggressive dink, not from off-bounce speed-ups
- **Strategy 2 — Take time away and create space**: volley-dinking (hitting balls out of the air rather than letting them bounce) applies time pressure; the combination of placement + time removal forces opponents to move more than you; spread opponents wide to create open middle, then attack the gap; the volley dink + fake pattern: aggressive dink one way, volley back where they came from (faking), then cross — splits them and creates "double jeopardy"
- **Strategy 3 — Control the middle**: "middle solves the riddle"; hitting to the middle (1) removes opponent angles, (2) creates communication confusion about who takes the ball, (3) jams opponents at the inside foot — one of the hardest dink positions to handle; when in a defensive position, reset to the middle to neutralize pressure AND create the inside-foot problem simultaneously
- **Role reframe**: stop playing "the bully" (trying to overpower); start playing "the strategic mastermind"; embracing this role makes the game more dangerous, not less

## Visual Notes

Filmed with three women demonstrating the drills at kitchen-line level. The foot-targeting drill clearly shows the difference between a dink at the hip versus one at the feet — the lower ball forces the receiver into a cramped, upward-hitting position. The volley-dink + fake sequence is shown as a three-step combo: pull wide, volley back where they came from, change direction.
