# My 9-Year-Old Plays at a 4.0 Level — Here's How

**Type:** YouTube instructional / training footage
**Date:** 2026-05-21
**Channel:** [Briones Pickleball Academy](../entities/briones-pickleball-academy.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=88LjSQfXW3A
**Duration:** 17:30

## Key Takeaways

- **Subject**: Josiah Briones, age 9, Jordan Briones's son; began pickleball at age 7; reached 4.0-level play in approximately 2 years of training
- **Three keys to reach 4.0** (also applicable to all adult players trying to advance):
  1. **Counter-attacks** — the ability to handle balls to the dominant side, body, and non-dominant side without getting caught with a "chicken wing"; drill: three-ball counter drill where the feeder doesn't telegraph location; Josiah's cue: neutral paddle position (not resting on the backhand side), which allows quick movement either direction
  2. **Consistent dinks** — topspin forehand dink with shuffle step footwork for aggressive situations; "shovel/lift" motion for when you're reaching or out of position (shoulder-only, less foot movement needed, higher consistency); two-handed backhand dink for the left side (provides power and topspin; sets up middle attacks and put-aways); slice/lift backhand for defensive situations where topspin isn't achievable
  3. **Transition game** — drive-drop strategy: drive at 65% pace to apply pressure (not to win the point), which forces the returner to volley → shorter ball → easier drop → advance to kitchen; topspin drops for weaker returns that sit up; reset shots in transition for deep hard returns when driving isn't viable
- **Josiah's words on the drop**: "Sometimes their counters are really good so I'll just drop it and come in" — demonstrates mature third-shot decision-making despite age 9
- **Two-handed backhand dink**: Josiah cites power as the reason; Jordan's pedagogical point is that it sets up middle balls and creates put-away opportunities

## Visual Notes

Real-time training footage shows Josiah hitting heavy topspin dinks that land at Jordan's feet, forcing him to half-volley. The drive-drop sequence is shown at full speed — compact 65% drive, immediate split-stop, then soft drop to kitchen. Josiah's ready position is notably wide-based and low for his size.
