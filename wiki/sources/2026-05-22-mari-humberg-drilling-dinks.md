# Drilling with Intention Part 1: DINKS

**Type:** YouTube instructional / drilling footage
**Date:** 2026-05-22
**Channel:** [Mari Humberg](../entities/mari-humberg.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TNwyU5qGbgY
**Duration:** 9:13

## Key Takeaways

- **Mari's ideal training day**: 2-hour morning drilling session, 2-hour afternoon playing session, gym session; she starts mornings with drilling because "that's when I get my rhythm and actually feel the ball" — mornings for skill building, afternoons for implementation
- **Dink consistency drill — the 100-rep sequence**: 100 dinks in a row in each direction (down the line from left, down the line from right, crosscourt left, crosscourt right); cannot progress to next direction until 100 achieved; Mari's personal standard is 100 in each; she suggests adjusting based on skill level
- **Quality over comfort**: dinks must be match-quality — low, intentional, moving the opponent, with footwork; passive lazy dinks that never miss do nothing; both partners should be working the ball and getting out of breath at 100 reps
- **Crosscourt drill nuance**: don't push partner so far toward the middle that they're practicing taking middle balls with their backhand — that creates bad habits; keep dinks genuinely crosscourt, not pushing into the center
- **Gamification with stakes**: add consequences to keep pressure in the drill (e.g., "butts up" for misses, or 3-lives system — lose three lives and restart)
- **Dink game (no attacks)**: play game to 5 or 11 against your partner where no attacks are allowed regardless of ball height; this forces players to work on controlling high balls, rolling, and shaping shots rather than just speeding up; great warm-up for doubles teams before match play; drilling partner AB consistently pushes Mari, who misses when she overstretches — demonstrates even elite players need intentional restraint

## Visual Notes

Both players are visibly moving their feet on every dink — shuffle step for wide balls, small adjustment steps for balls near body; never stationary during the sequence. The dink game shows Mari hitting aggressive topspin dinks that land 6–8 inches from the net, forcing AB into cramped positions. When AB gets a high ball, the constraint prevents attack — she works on rolling it back low instead.
