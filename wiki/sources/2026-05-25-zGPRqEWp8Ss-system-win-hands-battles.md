# I Figured Out a System to Win 99% of Hands Battles

**Source type:** YouTube video
**Channel:** [Pickleball Playbook](../entities/pickleballplaybook.md)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zGPRqEWp8Ss
**Uploaded:** 2026-05-25

## Key Takeaways

- **Three drilling progressions for hands battles:** (1) Double-hit volleys — players alternate hitting two volleys before passing to partner, training paddle recovery; (2) Romanian volleys — players volley with an open base while moving laterally, training hands speed while in motion; (3) Hills up volleys — start close (1 foot from partner), one step back after each successful exchange, reaching maximum distance then returning — simulates varying pace and angle.
- **Five system tips:** (1) Ready position reset after every shot; (2) Wide base always — feet wider than shoulder-width at the kitchen; (3) Elbows out — never tucked; (4) Give space — when the ball comes hard, absorb back slightly rather than meeting force with force; (5) Choke up on the paddle handle for faster hand exchanges.
- **Give space = counter-intuitive key:** Most players instinctively push forward when attacked hard. The correct move is to slightly absorb backward, creating space for the paddle to move without full-arm compensation. This is the hardest tip to implement under pressure.
- **Romanian volley insight:** Moving laterally during volley exchanges (as in Romanian volleys) is harder than standing still because the feet are not set at contact. Training this makes the stationary hands battle feel easy by comparison.
- **Gabe Tardio reference:** Austin mentions Gabe Tardio as an elite example of the choke-up grip at the kitchen — film study of Tardio's hand position during hands battles confirms the handle grip is 1–2 inches above normal.
